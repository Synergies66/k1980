中伊联手挑战美元霸权

China and Iran Join Forces to Challenge US Dollar Hegemony

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月09日
中国与伊朗在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过石油贸易绕过美元结算体系。这一举动可能加速去美元化进程，影响全球货币格局、投资组合配置和能源价格稳定。对投资者而言，需关注人民币资产配置机会和美元资产风险。
China and Iran are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, bypassing the US dollar settlement system through oil trade. This move may accelerate de-dollarization, affecting global currency patterns, portfolio allocation, and energy price stability. Investors should pay attention to opportunities in renminbi asset allocation and risks in US dollar assets.

霍尔木兹海峡的金融博弈

霍尔木兹海峡作为全球石油运输的咽喉要道，如今正成为货币战争的新战场。中国与伊朗正在这一战略要地联手挑战美元在国际贸易中的主导地位，这一举动的影响远超地缘政治范畴。

去美元化的现实路径

中伊两国通过石油贸易绕过美元结算体系，主要采用人民币和易货贸易方式。这种模式为其他受美国制裁影响的国家提供了可行的替代方案。俄乌冲突后，越来越多国家开始寻求减少对美元的依赖，中伊合作模式具有重要示范效应。

对普通投资者而言，这意味着全球储备货币格局可能面临重构。美元指数的长期走势可能受到冲击，而人民币等其他货币的国际地位有望提升。

对投资组合的直接影响

首先是汇率波动风险加大。美元霸权受到挑战可能导致美元相对贬值，持有美元资产的投资者需要重新评估风险敞口。同时，人民币资产的配置价值上升，包括中国债券市场和A股市场。

其次是大宗商品价格体系变化。传统上以美元计价的石油可能出现更多元化的定价机制，这将影响能源类投资产品的表现。投资者需要关注油价波动对通胀预期的影响。

对职业发展的启示

金融服务业从业者需要关注跨境支付和结算领域的变化。数字货币、区块链技术在绕过传统金融体系方面的应用将加速发展，相关技能需求上升。

国际贸易从业者面临新的机遇和挑战。掌握多元化结算方式、了解不同货币体系将成为核心竞争力。特别是在能源、大宗商品贸易领域，专业人才需求将显著增长。

移民和资产配置考量

对于考虑海外资产配置的投资者，地缘政治风险成为重要考量因素。传统的美元避险天堂地位可能受到冲击，需要更加多元化的配置策略。

加拿大、新加坡等中立国家的资产配置价值上升。这些国家既与美国保持良好关系，又在去美元化趋势中保持相对中立的立场。

风险与机遇并存

短期内，这种挑战可能加剧国际金融市场波动。美国可能采取更严厉的制裁措施，导致全球贸易摩擦加剧。投资者需要做好应对高波动性的准备。

长期而言，多极化的国际货币体系可能更加稳定和公平。这为新兴市场国家提供了更多发展空间，也为投资者提供了新的增长机会。

实际应对策略

对于个人投资者，建议适度增加非美元资产配置，包括人民币债券、黄金等传统避险资产，以及新兴市场股票。同时，密切关注央行数字货币的发展动态。

对于企业，特别是从事国际贸易的公司，需要建立多元化的结算体系，降低对单一货币的依赖。提前布局人民币结算能力将成为竞争优势。

中伊在霍尔木兹海峡的合作只是全球金融格局变化的开始。这一趋势将深刻影响每个人的财富配置和职业发展，需要我们以更加开放和灵活的心态应对这一历史性变革。

Financial Game at the Strait of Hormuz

The Strait of Hormuz, serving as the vital artery for global oil transportation, is now becoming a new battleground for currency wars. China and Iran are joining forces at this strategic location to challenge the dominance of the US dollar in international trade, an action with implications far beyond geopolitics.

The Practical Path to De-dollarization

China and Iran bypass the US dollar settlement system through oil trade, primarily using renminbi and barter trade methods. This model provides a viable alternative for other countries affected by US sanctions. Following the Russia-Ukraine conflict, an increasing number of countries are seeking to reduce their dependence on the US dollar, making the China-Iran cooperation model of significant demonstration value.

For ordinary investors, this suggests that the global reserve currency landscape may face restructuring. The long-term trajectory of the US dollar index may be impacted, while the international status of other currencies such as the renminbi is expected to rise.

Direct Impact on Investment Portfolios

First, exchange rate volatility risks increase. A challenge to US dollar hegemony may lead to relative dollar depreciation, requiring investors holding dollar assets to reassess their risk exposure. Meanwhile, the allocation value of renminbi assets rises, including Chinese bond markets and A-share markets.

Second, there are changes in commodity pricing systems. Crude oil, traditionally priced in US dollars, may develop more diversified pricing mechanisms, affecting the performance of energy investment products. Investors need to monitor how oil price fluctuations impact inflation expectations.

Implications for Career Development

Financial services professionals need to pay attention to changes in cross-border payment and settlement fields. Applications of digital currencies and blockchain technology in bypassing traditional financial systems will accelerate, creating increased demand for related skills.

International trade professionals face new opportunities and challenges. Mastering diversified settlement methods and understanding different currency systems will become core competencies. Particularly in energy and commodity trading sectors, demand for specialized talent will grow significantly.

Considerations for Immigration and Asset Allocation

For investors considering overseas asset allocation, geopolitical risk becomes an important consideration. The traditional status of the US dollar as a safe haven may be undermined, requiring more diversified allocation strategies.

The allocation value of assets in neutral countries like Canada and Singapore rises. These countries maintain good relations with the United States while maintaining relatively neutral positions in the de-dollarization trend.

Risks and Opportunities Coexist

In the short term, such challenges may intensify volatility in international financial markets. The United States may adopt stricter sanctions measures, leading to increased global trade friction. Investors should be prepared for high volatility.

In the long term, a multipolar international monetary system may prove more stable and equitable. This provides more development space for emerging market countries and creates new growth opportunities for investors.

Practical Response Strategies

For individual investors, it is recommended to moderately increase non-dollar asset allocation, including renminbi bonds, gold and other traditional safe-haven assets, as well as emerging market stocks. Meanwhile, closely monitor developments in central bank digital currencies.

For enterprises, particularly those engaged in international trade, there is a need to establish diversified settlement systems to reduce dependence on a single currency. Early implementation of renminbi settlement capabilities will become a competitive advantage.

China and Iran's cooperation at the Strait of Hormuz is only the beginning of changes in the global financial landscape. This trend will profoundly affect everyone's wealth allocation and career development, requiring us to respond to this historic transformation with a more open and flexible mindset.
