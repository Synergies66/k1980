中伊挑战美元霸权，全球金融格局将重塑

China and Iran Challenge Dollar Hegemony, Global Financial Landscape to be Reshaped

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月13日
中国与伊朗在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过石油贸易绕过美元结算体系，直接冲击美元在国际贸易中的主导地位。这一举措可能引发全球货币体系变革，影响投资者资产配置、企业跨境贸易成本，以及各国汇率稳定性。
China and Iran strengthen cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, bypassing the US dollar settlement system through oil trade, directly challenging the dollar's dominant position in international trade. This initiative may trigger a global monetary system transformation, affecting investors' asset allocation, cross-border trade costs for enterprises, and exchange rate stability across nations.

美元霸权面临严峻挑战

中国与伊朗在霍尔木兹海峡的最新合作，标志着对美元国际储备货币地位的直接挑战。霍尔木兹海峡作为全球石油运输的咽喉要道，承载着世界约20%的石油贸易量。中伊两国选择在此关键区域推进去美元化合作，其象征意义和实际影响都不容小觑。

伊朗长期受到美国制裁，被排除在SWIFT国际结算体系之外，这迫使其寻找替代性支付方式。中国作为全球第二大经济体和最大石油进口国，与伊朗的能源合作为双方提供了绕过美元体系的现实路径。

对投资者的直接影响

这一趋势将对全球投资格局产生深远影响。首先，美元资产的避险属性可能受到质疑。随着更多国家寻求贸易结算的多元化，美元需求可能逐步下降，影响美元汇率走势和美债收益率。

投资者需要重新评估货币风险敞口。人民币、欧元等其他主要货币的国际地位可能提升，相关资产配置价值增加。同时，黄金等传统避险资产可能重新受到青睐，因为在多极化货币体系中，黄金的中性储备价值更加凸显。

石油及能源板块投资逻辑也将发生变化。中伊合作可能带来新的能源供应链和定价机制，投资者需要关注相关上市公司的地缘政治风险敞口。

企业运营成本重构

对于跨国企业而言，去美元化趋势意味着财务管理复杂性的增加。企业需要建立多币种结算体系，增加汇率对冲成本。特别是与中东地区有业务往来的公司，可能需要重新设计支付流程和风险管控机制。

中国企业在这一变化中可能获得相对优势。人民币国际化进程的加速，将降低中国企业的汇兑成本，提高在国际市场的竞争力。同时，也为中国金融机构拓展海外业务提供了新机遇。

地缘政治风险升级

美国不会坐视美元霸权受到挑战。预计美国可能通过加强制裁、施压盟友等方式进行反制。这种博弈将增加全球市场的不确定性，投资者需要密切关注相关政策动向。

欧盟等其他经济体可能在中美博弈中寻求平衡，推动建立更加多元化的国际货币体系。这一过程中，新兴市场货币的地位可能得到提升。

应对策略建议

个人投资者应适度分散货币风险，不要过度集中持有单一货币资产。关注人民币国际化相关投资机会，如离岸人民币债券、跨境ETF等产品。

企业应提前布局多币种结算能力，建立灵活的汇率风险管理机制。特别是能源、大宗商品贸易企业，需要评估新兴支付体系的可行性。

长期来看，全球货币体系的多极化是不可逆转的趋势。这既带来挑战，也创造了新的机遇。关键是要保持敏锐的市场嗅觉，及时调整策略以适应变化。

US Dollar Hegemony Faces Severe Challenges

The latest cooperation between China and Iran in the Strait of Hormuz marks a direct challenge to the dollar's position as an international reserve currency. The Strait of Hormuz, serving as the critical gateway for global oil transportation, handles approximately 20% of the world's oil trade volume. The choice by China and Iran to advance de-dollarization cooperation in this strategic region carries both symbolic and practical significance that cannot be overlooked.

Iran has long faced US sanctions and been excluded from the SWIFT international settlement system, forcing it to seek alternative payment methods. China, as the world's second-largest economy and largest oil importer, has found a practical path to bypass the dollar system through energy cooperation with Iran.

Direct Impact on Investors

This trend will have far-reaching implications for the global investment landscape. First, the safe-haven attributes of dollar assets may be questioned. As more countries seek diversified trade settlement options, dollar demand may gradually decline, affecting dollar exchange rates and US Treasury yields.

Investors need to reassess their currency risk exposure. The international status of other major currencies such as the renminbi and euro may be elevated, increasing the value of related asset allocations. Meanwhile, traditional safe-haven assets like gold may regain favor, as gold's neutral reserve value becomes more prominent in a multipolar currency system.

The investment logic for oil and energy sectors will also shift. China-Iran cooperation may bring new energy supply chains and pricing mechanisms, requiring investors to monitor the geopolitical risk exposure of related listed companies.

Corporate Operating Costs Restructured

For multinational enterprises, the de-dollarization trend means increased complexity in financial management. Companies need to establish multi-currency settlement systems and increase foreign exchange hedging costs. Particularly those with business operations in the Middle East may need to redesign payment processes and risk management mechanisms.

Chinese enterprises may gain relative advantages in this change. The acceleration of renminbi internationalization will reduce foreign exchange conversion costs for Chinese enterprises, enhancing their competitiveness in international markets. It also creates new opportunities for Chinese financial institutions to expand overseas business.

Geopolitical Risk Escalation

The United States will not sit idly by as its dollar hegemony is challenged. The US is expected to retaliate through enhanced sanctions and pressure on allies. This competition will increase uncertainty in global markets, requiring investors to closely monitor relevant policy developments.

Other economic entities such as the European Union may seek balance in the China-US competition, promoting the establishment of a more diversified international monetary system. In this process, the status of emerging market currencies may be elevated.

Recommended Response Strategies

Individual investors should moderately diversify currency risks and avoid over-concentrating holdings in a single currency. Focus on investment opportunities related to renminbi internationalization, such as offshore renminbi bonds and cross-border ETF products.

Enterprises should proactively develop multi-currency settlement capabilities and establish flexible foreign exchange risk management mechanisms. Particularly for energy and commodity trading enterprises, it is necessary to assess the feasibility of emerging payment systems.

In the long term, the multipolarization of the global monetary system is an irreversible trend. This presents both challenges and new opportunities. The key is to maintain keen market awareness and adjust strategies promptly to adapt to changes.
