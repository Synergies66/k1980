伊朗中国挑战美元霸权 投资者需关注风险

Iran and China Challenge US Dollar Hegemony: Investors Need to Pay Attention to Risks

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月16日
伊朗和中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过能源贸易绕过美元结算体系，直接挑战美元霸权地位。此举将重塑全球金融格局，影响汇率稳定、大宗商品价格和投资组合配置，投资者需密切关注地缘政治风险对全球市场的连锁反应。
Iran and China are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, bypassing the US dollar settlement system through energy trade and directly challenging the dollar's hegemonic position. This move will reshape the global financial landscape and impact exchange rate stability, commodity prices, and investment portfolio allocation. Investors need to closely monitor the spillover effects of geopolitical risks on global markets.

地缘政治重塑金融版图

伊朗与中国在战略要地霍尔木兹海峡的最新合作动向，标志着全球金融秩序正面临前所未有的挑战。作为控制全球约20%石油运输的关键水道，霍尔木兹海峡的任何变化都将对国际市场产生深远影响。两国通过建立双边贸易机制，绕过传统的美元结算体系，这不仅是经济合作，更是对既有国际金融架构的直接冲击。

能源贸易去美元化趋势

中伊两国在能源领域的深度合作正在改写游戏规则。中国作为全球最大石油进口国，与伊朗这一重要产油国建立人民币结算机制，将显著减少对美元的依赖。这种"石油人民币"模式已在其他国家得到推广，俄罗斯、沙特等国也在探索类似合作。对投资者而言，这意味着传统的"石油美元"循环正在松动，可能导致美元需求下降，进而影响美元汇率和美国国债收益率。

投资组合重新配置策略

面对这一趋势，投资者需要重新审视资产配置策略。首先，黄金等避险资产可能受益于美元霸权的削弱，成为对冲通胀和货币贬值的重要工具。其次，新兴市场货币，特别是人民币资产，可能获得更多关注。中国债券市场的国际化进程将加速，为全球投资者提供美元资产的替代选择。

能源板块投资也面临新机遇。随着东西方能源贸易路径的多元化，亚洲能源基础设施、清洁能源技术以及相关物流企业可能迎来发展机遇。投资者应关注参与"一带一路"倡议的企业，这些公司将直接受益于新兴贸易走廊的建设。

汇率市场波动加剧

去美元化进程将显著影响外汇市场格局。美元指数可能面临长期下行压力，而人民币、欧元等主要货币的国际地位有望提升。对于持有美元资产的投资者，需要考虑汇率风险对投资收益的侵蚀。同时，新兴市场货币的波动性可能增加，需要更加精细化的风险管理。

跨国企业的财务管理也将面临挑战。随着多币种结算体系的发展，企业需要建立更加灵活的外汇对冲策略，降低汇率波动对经营业绩的冲击。

科技金融基础设施变革

数字货币和跨境支付技术正成为这场变革的重要推手。中国的数字人民币试点、伊朗的加密货币探索，以及两国在区块链技术方面的合作，都在为绕过传统美元体系提供技术支撑。金融科技企业，特别是专注于跨境支付、数字货币基础设施的公司，可能迎来新的发展机遇。

风险与机遇并存

投资者需要认识到，这一进程充满不确定性。美国可能通过加强制裁、技术限制等手段进行反制，导致市场波动加剧。短期内，地缘政治紧张可能推高风险溢价，影响全球资本流动。

然而，长期来看，国际金融体系的多极化趋势不可逆转。投资者应当采用更加多元化的投资策略，关注不同地区、不同货币资产的配置平衡。同时，加强对地缘政治风险的监测，及时调整投资策略。

应对策略建议

建议投资者密切关注相关政策动向，适度增加黄金、大宗商品等实物资产配置，考虑投资亚洲基础设施、清洁能源等受益行业，同时保持适当的流动性以应对市场波动。这场金融秩序的重构正在进行，准备充分的投资者将能够在变局中寻找到新的机遇。

Geopolitics Reshaping Financial Geography

The latest cooperation developments between Iran and China at the strategic chokepoint of the Strait of Hormuz signal that the global financial order is facing unprecedented challenges. As a critical waterway controlling approximately 20% of global oil transportation, any changes in the Strait of Hormuz will have far-reaching impacts on international markets. By establishing bilateral trade mechanisms, the two countries are bypassing the traditional US dollar settlement system—this is not merely economic cooperation, but a direct blow to the existing international financial architecture.

De-Dollarization Trend in Energy Trade

The deep cooperation between China and Iran in the energy sector is rewriting the rules of the game. As the world's largest oil importer, China's establishment of a yuan settlement mechanism with Iran, a major oil-producing nation, will significantly reduce dependence on the US dollar. This "petro-yuan" model has already been promoted in other countries, with Russia, Saudi Arabia, and others exploring similar cooperation. For investors, this means the traditional "petrodollar" cycle is loosening, potentially leading to decreased dollar demand and affecting the dollar exchange rate and US Treasury yields.

Investment Portfolio Reconfiguration Strategy

Faced with this trend, investors need to reassess their asset allocation strategies. First, safe-haven assets like gold may benefit from the weakening of dollar hegemony, becoming an important tool to hedge against inflation and currency depreciation. Second, emerging market currencies, particularly yuan-denominated assets, may attract more attention. The internationalization process of China's bond market will accelerate, providing global investors with alternatives to dollar assets.

Energy sector investments also face new opportunities. With the diversification of energy trade routes between East and West, Asian energy infrastructure, clean energy technology, and related logistics companies may encounter development opportunities. Investors should focus on companies participating in the "Belt and Road" initiative, as these firms will directly benefit from the construction of emerging trade corridors.

Increased Foreign Exchange Market Volatility

The de-dollarization process will significantly affect the structure of the foreign exchange market. The US Dollar Index may face long-term downward pressure, while the international status of major currencies like the yuan and euro is expected to rise. For investors holding dollar-denominated assets, exchange rate risks' erosion of investment returns must be considered. Meanwhile, the volatility of emerging market currencies may increase, requiring more refined risk management.

Financial management of multinational enterprises will also face challenges. With the development of multi-currency settlement systems, companies need to establish more flexible foreign exchange hedging strategies to mitigate the impact of exchange rate fluctuations on operational performance.

Transformation of Financial Technology Infrastructure

Digital currencies and cross-border payment technologies are becoming important drivers of this change. China's digital yuan pilot, Iran's cryptocurrency exploration, and cooperation between the two countries in blockchain technology are all providing technical support for bypassing the traditional dollar system. Financial technology companies, particularly those focused on cross-border payments and digital currency infrastructure, may encounter new development opportunities.

Risks and Opportunities Coexist

Investors need to recognize that this process is fraught with uncertainty. The United States may take countermeasures through strengthened sanctions, technology restrictions, and other means, leading to increased market volatility. In the short term, geopolitical tensions may push up risk premiums and affect global capital flows.

However, in the long term, the multipolar trend in the international financial system is irreversible. Investors should adopt more diversified investment strategies, focusing on balanced allocation across different regions and currency assets. At the same time, strengthen monitoring of geopolitical risks and adjust investment strategies promptly.

Recommended Response Strategies

Investors are advised to closely monitor relevant policy developments, moderately increase allocation to physical assets such as gold and commodities, consider investing in beneficiary sectors like Asian infrastructure and clean energy, and maintain appropriate liquidity to cope with market volatility. This financial restructuring is underway, and well-prepared investors will be able to find new opportunities in this changing landscape.
