伊朗中国挑战美元霸权，全球金融格局巨变

Iran and China Challenge Dollar Hegemony, Global Financial Landscape Undergoes Massive Changes

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月13日
伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过石油贸易去美元化等方式挑战美元霸权地位。这一趋势可能重塑全球金融体系，影响投资组合配置、汇率走势和国际贸易结算方式，投资者需关注新兴支付体系和货币多元化带来的机遇与风险。
Iran and China are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region and challenging the dollar's hegemonic position through methods such as de-dollarization of oil trade. This trend may reshape the global financial system, affecting portfolio allocation, exchange rate movements, and international trade settlement methods. Investors need to pay attention to both opportunities and risks brought by emerging payment systems and currency diversification.

美元霸权面临前所未有挑战

在全球地缘政治格局深刻变化的背景下，伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡这一全球能源运输要道的合作，正在对美元主导的国际金融体系构成直接挑战。这不仅是两国间的双边合作，更是对现有国际货币秩序的系统性冲击。

霍尔木兹海峡控制着全球约20%的石油运输，伊朗凭借地理优势与中国这一全球第二大经济体结盟，通过石油贸易去美元化，正在构建绕过美国金融制裁的替代性支付体系。

投资组合重新配置的必要性

对于投资者而言，这一趋势带来了资产配置策略的根本性调整需求。传统以美元为核心的投资组合可能面临系统性风险。投资者应考虑增加人民币、欧元等非美货币资产的配置比例。

黄金作为传统避险资产，在去美元化进程中可能获得新的支撑。同时，与新兴支付体系相关的数字货币、区块链技术公司也值得关注。能源板块，特别是与中东地区有密切合作的企业，可能从新的贸易结算方式中受益。

汇率市场的结构性变化

美元指数可能面临长期下行压力。人民币国际化进程将加速，特别是在能源贸易领域的使用将显著增加。这对从事国际贸易的企业意味着汇率风险管理策略需要调整，多货币对冲变得更加重要。

对于有海外投资需求的个人投资者，应密切关注汇率走势，适时调整外汇配置。持有美元资产的投资者需要评估美元长期贬值的风险。

国际贸易结算方式革命

传统以SWIFT系统为主导的国际支付体系正在面临挑战。中国的CIPS（人民币跨境支付系统）使用范围不断扩大，俄罗斯的SPFS系统也在发挥作用。企业在国际贸易中将有更多支付选择，但也需要适应更加复杂的结算环境。

这对跨国公司的财务管理提出了新要求，需要建立多元化的支付渠道和风险管控机制。中小企业可能需要寻找新的金融服务提供商，以适应多元化的国际结算需求。

就业市场的新机遇

金融科技、国际贸易、风险管理等领域将迎来新的发展机遇。掌握多种货币体系、了解新兴支付技术的专业人才需求将大幅增加。

传统银行业将面临转型压力，需要开发新的产品和服务以适应多元化的国际支付需求。这为金融从业者提供了新的职业发展方向。

签证和国际流动影响

货币体系的多元化可能影响国际资本流动和人员往来。中国与伊朗等国的合作加强，可能为相关领域的商务往来和技术交流创造新机会。

但同时，美国等西方国家可能加强对相关人员和资金流动的监管，这需要相关人员在国际往来中更加注意合规风险。

应对策略建议

面对这一历史性变革，个人和企业都需要制定相应的应对策略。投资者应坚持多元化原则，避免过度依赖单一货币或市场。企业应建立灵活的财务管理体系，适应多元化的国际支付环境。

同时，密切关注相关政策变化和市场动向，及时调整策略以应对可能的风险和机遇。这场去美元化的进程虽然充满不确定性，但也为有准备的投资者和企业创造了新的发展空间。

Dollar Hegemony Faces Unprecedented Challenge

Against the backdrop of profound changes in the global geopolitical landscape, cooperation between Iran and China in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical global energy transportation route, is directly challenging the U.S. dollar-dominated international financial system. This is not merely bilateral cooperation between the two countries, but a systemic shock to the existing international monetary order.

The Strait of Hormuz controls approximately 20% of global oil transportation. Leveraging its geographical advantages and aligning with China, the world's second-largest economy, Iran is pursuing de-dollarization of oil trade and constructing an alternative payment system that bypasses U.S. financial sanctions.

Necessity of Portfolio Reallocation

For investors, this trend requires fundamental adjustments to asset allocation strategies. Traditional investment portfolios centered on the U.S. dollar may face systemic risks. Investors should consider increasing their allocation of non-dollar assets such as the Chinese yuan and euro.

Gold, as a traditional safe-haven asset, may gain new support in the de-dollarization process. Meanwhile, digital currencies and blockchain technology companies related to emerging payment systems warrant attention. The energy sector, particularly enterprises with close cooperation in the Middle East, may benefit from new trade settlement methods.

Structural Changes in Foreign Exchange Markets

The U.S. dollar index may face long-term downward pressure. The internationalization process of the Chinese yuan will accelerate, particularly in energy trade. For enterprises engaged in international trade, this means foreign exchange risk management strategies need adjustment, with multi-currency hedging becoming increasingly important.

Individual investors with overseas investment needs should closely monitor exchange rate trends and adjust foreign exchange allocations timely. Those holding dollar assets need to assess the risk of long-term dollar depreciation.

Revolution in International Trade Settlement Methods

The traditional international payment system dominated by the SWIFT system is facing challenges. The scope of China's CIPS (Cross-border Interbank Payment System) is continuously expanding, and Russia's SPFS system is also playing a role. Enterprises in international trade will have more payment options but need to adapt to a more complex settlement environment.

This poses new requirements for financial management in multinational corporations, requiring the establishment of diversified payment channels and risk control mechanisms. Small and medium-sized enterprises may need to seek new financial service providers to accommodate diversified international settlement needs.

New Opportunities in the Job Market

Fields such as fintech, international trade, and risk management will welcome new development opportunities. Demand for professional talent proficient in multiple currency systems and familiar with emerging payment technologies will surge significantly.

Traditional banking will face transformation pressure, requiring development of new products and services to adapt to diversified international payment needs. This provides new career development directions for financial professionals.

Impact on Visas and International Mobility

Diversification of currency systems may affect international capital flows and personnel mobility. Strengthened cooperation between China and Iran and other nations may create new opportunities for business activities and technical exchanges in related fields.

However, simultaneously, the United States and other Western countries may strengthen regulations on related personnel and capital flows, requiring those engaged in international activities to be more attentive to compliance risks.

Recommended Response Strategies

Facing this historic transformation, both individuals and enterprises need to formulate corresponding response strategies. Investors should adhere to diversification principles, avoiding over-reliance on any single currency or market. Enterprises should establish flexible financial management systems adapted to diversified international payment environments.

Simultaneously, closely monitor relevant policy changes and market movements, adjusting strategies timely to address potential risks and opportunities. Although the de-dollarization process is fraught with uncertainty, it also creates new development space for well-prepared investors and enterprises.
