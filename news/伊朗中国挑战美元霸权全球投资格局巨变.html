伊朗中国挑战美元霸权：全球投资格局巨变

Iran and China Challenge Dollar Hegemony: Global Investment Landscape Undergoes Massive Shift

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月08日
伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过能源贸易绕过美元结算体系，直接冲击美元在国际贸易中的主导地位。这一趋势将重塑全球金融格局，影响汇率稳定、投资组合配置和国际贸易成本。对个人而言，需关注人民币资产配置机会，警惕美元资产波动风险，同时留意能源价格变化对生活成本的影响。
Iran and China are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz, bypassing the dollar settlement system through energy trade, directly challenging the dollar's dominant position in international commerce. This trend will reshape the global financial landscape, affecting exchange rate stability, investment portfolio allocation, and international trade costs. For individuals, attention should be paid to renminbi asset allocation opportunities, awareness of dollar asset volatility risks, and monitoring of energy price changes' impact on living costs.

去美元化浪潮正在加速

伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡的合作标志着全球去美元化进程进入新阶段。作为全球最重要的石油运输通道，霍尔木兹海峡承载着全球约20%的石油贸易。两国绕过美元进行能源交易，不仅是对美国制裁的回应，更是对现有国际金融秩序的直接挑战。

这种合作模式正在被更多国家效仿。俄罗斯、印度、巴西等新兴经济体也在推动本币结算，减少对美元的依赖。国际货币基金组织数据显示，美元在全球外汇储备中的占比已从2000年的71%下降至目前的59%左右。

对全球投资市场的冲击

美元霸权地位的松动将带来深远的投资影响。首先，美元指数可能面临长期下行压力，这将直接影响以美元计价的资产价格。美国国债作为全球最重要的避险资产，其需求可能减少，收益率走势将更加复杂。

同时，人民币国际化进程将明显加速。中国人民银行数据显示，人民币在国际支付中的份额已升至4.5%，创历史新高。随着更多国家接受人民币结算，人民币资产的配置价值将显著提升。

大宗商品市场也将迎来重构。传统上以美元定价的石油、黄金等商品，可能出现多元化定价机制。这将增加价格波动的复杂性，但也为投资者提供了新的套利机会。

企业运营成本重新洗牌

对跨国企业而言，支付体系的多元化既是挑战也是机遇。传统依赖SWIFT系统进行国际结算的企业，需要适应新的支付通道，如中国的CIPS系统或其他区域性结算网络。

汇率风险管理变得更加复杂。企业需要对冲更多种货币的汇率波动，这将增加财务管理成本，但也可能通过多元化降低单一货币风险。能源密集型企业可能受益于更加灵活的定价机制，特别是那些能够直接与产油国建立本币贸易关系的企业。

个人投资策略调整指南

面对这一变局，个人投资者需要重新审视资产配置策略。建议适当增加人民币资产配置，包括人民币债券、A股市场和人民币计价的理财产品。同时，可以关注受益于去美元化趋势的相关概念股，如跨境支付、数字货币技术公司。

黄金等实物资产的避险价值将进一步凸显。在货币体系重构过程中，黄金作为超主权货币的地位可能得到强化，值得作为投资组合的重要组成部分。

对于有海外投资需求的投资者，需要更加关注汇率变化。美元资产虽然面临挑战，但在相当长时间内仍将保持重要地位，关键是把握配置比例和时机。

生活成本影响预判

普通消费者最直接的感受可能来自能源价格变化。如果去美元化进程顺利，可能会降低美国通过美元政策影响全球能源价格的能力，有助于价格稳定。但过渡期内的不确定性可能导致价格波动加剧。

汇率变化也会影响进口商品价格。美元走弱可能降低以美元计价的进口商品成本，但也要警惕其他货币升值带来的新的价格压力。

长期趋势展望

伊朗中国的合作只是去美元化进程的一个缩影。随着更多国家加入这一行列，国际金融体系将逐步向多极化演进。这个过程可能持续10-20年，期间将伴随较大的不确定性和波动性。

投资者和企业需要培养适应多元化货币体系的能力，这不仅是风险管理的需要，更是把握新机遇的关键。在这个历史性转折点上，保持敏锐的市场洞察力和灵活的应对策略至关重要。

The De-Dollarization Wave is Accelerating

The cooperation between Iran and China in the Strait of Hormuz marks a new stage in the global de-dollarization process. As the world's most critical oil transportation corridor, the Strait of Hormuz carries approximately 20% of global oil trade. By bypassing the dollar for energy transactions, the two countries are not only responding to US sanctions but directly challenging the existing international financial order.

This cooperation model is being emulated by more countries. Emerging economies such as Russia, India, and Brazil are also promoting local currency settlement and reducing dependence on the dollar. According to International Monetary Fund data, the dollar's share in global foreign exchange reserves has declined from 71% in 2000 to approximately 59% currently.

Impact on Global Investment Markets

The erosion of dollar hegemony will have profound investment implications. First, the dollar index may face long-term downward pressure, directly affecting the prices of dollar-denominated assets. US Treasury bonds, as the world's most important safe-haven assets, may see reduced demand, making their yield trends more complex.

Meanwhile, the internationalization of the renminbi will accelerate significantly. According to People's Bank of China data, the renminbi's share in international payments has risen to 4.5%, reaching a historic high. As more countries accept renminbi settlement, the allocation value of renminbi assets will increase substantially.

The commodities market will also face restructuring. Commodities traditionally priced in dollars, such as oil and gold, may experience diversified pricing mechanisms. This will increase the complexity of price fluctuations but also provide investors with new arbitrage opportunities.

Corporate Operating Costs Realigned

For multinational enterprises, the diversification of payment systems presents both challenges and opportunities. Traditional enterprises relying on the SWIFT system for international settlement need to adapt to new payment channels, such as China's CIPS system or other regional settlement networks.

Foreign exchange risk management becomes more complex. Enterprises need to hedge against currency fluctuations of multiple currencies, which will increase financial management costs but may also reduce single-currency risks through diversification. Energy-intensive enterprises may benefit from more flexible pricing mechanisms, particularly those capable of establishing direct local currency trading relationships with oil-producing nations.

Personal Investment Strategy Adjustment Guide

Facing this shift, individual investors need to reassess their asset allocation strategies. It is recommended to moderately increase renminbi asset allocation, including renminbi bonds, A-share markets, and renminbi-denominated wealth management products. Simultaneously, attention can be paid to related concept stocks that benefit from de-dollarization trends, such as cross-border payment and digital currency technology companies.

The safe-haven value of physical assets like gold will be further enhanced. During the process of monetary system restructuring, gold's status as a super-sovereign currency may be reinforced, warranting its role as an important component of investment portfolios.

For investors with overseas investment needs, greater attention must be paid to exchange rate changes. Although dollar assets face challenges, they will likely maintain their important position for a considerable time, with the key being to grasp allocation ratios and timing.

Prediction of Living Cost Impact

Ordinary consumers may most directly feel the effects of energy price changes. If the de-dollarization process proceeds smoothly, it may reduce the US ability to influence global energy prices through dollar policy, helping to stabilize prices. However, uncertainties during the transition period may lead to increased price volatility.

Exchange rate changes will also affect import commodity prices. Dollar weakness may reduce the costs of dollar-denominated imported goods but should also guard against new price pressures from other currency appreciations.

Long-term Trend Outlook

The Iran-China cooperation is merely a microcosm of the de-dollarization process. As more countries join this trend, the international financial system will gradually evolve toward multipolarity. This process may last 10-20 years, accompanied by significant uncertainty and volatility.

Investors and enterprises need to develop the ability to adapt to a diversified currency system, which is not only necessary for risk management but also key to grasping new opportunities. At this historic turning point, maintaining keen market insight and flexible response strategies is of paramount importance.
