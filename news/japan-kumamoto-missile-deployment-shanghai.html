Japan Deploys Thousand-Kilometer Missiles in Kumamoto, Covering East China Sea to Shanghai

BBC 中文 · 2026年03月22日
日本首次在熊本部署射程1000公里长程导弹，突破以往300公里限制。该举措标志防务政策重大转变。
Japan deployed long-range missiles with a 1,000-kilometer range in Kumamoto for the first time, breaking through the previous 300-kilometer limit. This move marks a major shift in defense policy.

日本防卫省近期在熊本县部署了首批长程导弹系统，射程达到1000公里，这是日本军事历史上的重大突破。此前日本一直将导弹射程严格限制在300公里以内，符合其「专守防卫」的传统政策。

新部署的导弹系统覆盖范围包括东海海域、朝鲜半岛南部以及中国上海沿岸地区。军事分析人士认为，这一举措反映出日本防务策略正从被动防御向具备主动反击能力转变。

对于在日华人和频繁往来中日两国的商务人士而言，地区军事态势的变化可能影响未来的商贸往来和人员流动。专家建议密切关注后续政策发展，合理安排跨境业务和投资计划。

# Japan Deploys Long-Range Missile System in Major Military Shift

Japan's Ministry of Defense recently deployed its first long-range missile system in Kumamoto Prefecture, with a range of up to 1,000 kilometers, marking a major breakthrough in Japan's military history. Previously, Japan had strictly limited missile range to within 300 kilometers, in line with its traditional "exclusively defense-oriented" policy.

The newly deployed missile system's coverage area includes the East China Sea, southern Korean Peninsula, and coastal regions around Shanghai, China. Military analysts believe this move reflects a shift in Japan's defense strategy from passive defense to active counter-strike capability.

For Chinese residents in Japan and business professionals frequently traveling between China and Japan, changes in regional military posture may affect future trade and personnel mobility. Experts recommend closely monitoring subsequent policy developments and reasonably planning cross-border business operations and investment plans.
