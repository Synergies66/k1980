中伊挑战美元霸权，全球金融格局或生变

China and Iran Challenge Dollar Hegemony, Global Financial Landscape May Transform

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月14日
伊朗和中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区深化能源合作，推动以人民币结算石油贸易，直接挑战美元在国际储备货币中的主导地位。这一趋势可能重塑全球金融体系，影响汇率稳定、投资组合配置和跨境支付成本，投资者需重新评估货币风险和资产配置策略。
Iran and China are deepening energy cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz, promoting oil trade settlement in Chinese yuan, directly challenging the U.S. dollar's dominant position as the international reserve currency. This trend may reshape the global financial system, affecting exchange rate stability, portfolio allocation, and cross-border payment costs. Investors need to reassess currency risk and asset allocation strategies.

去美元化浪潮加速

中国和伊朗在霍尔木兹海峡的合作标志着全球去美元化进程的重要节点。伊朗作为全球第四大石油储量国，选择与中国深化以人民币计价的能源贸易，这不仅是两国规避美国制裁的策略，更是对布雷顿森林体系以来美元霸权的直接挑战。

霍尔木兹海峡控制着全球约20%的石油运输，这一地缘位置的战略价值使得中伊合作具有超越双边关系的全球意义。当越来越多的能源交易绕过美元体系，传统的石油美元循环机制将面临根本性冲击。

对投资者的直接影响

首先，汇率波动风险显著增加。美元地位的削弱可能导致其相对其他主要货币贬值，持有美元资产的投资者需要重新评估汇率对冲策略。同时，人民币国际化进程加速，可能为人民币资产带来新的投资机会。

其次，大宗商品定价机制面临重构。传统上以美元计价的石油、天然气等能源产品，如果越来越多地采用其他货币结算，将改变这些商品的价格发现机制，影响相关期货和衍生品市场。

全球金融体系重塑

去美元化趋势将推动多极化货币体系的形成。除了人民币，欧元、卢布等货币在国际贸易中的使用也在增加。这种变化将使全球金融体系更加复杂，但也可能更加稳定，因为风险不再过度集中于单一货币。

对于跨国企业而言，这意味着需要建立更加多元化的货币管理体系。企业可能需要持有多种货币的现金储备，建立更复杂的汇率风险管理机制，这将增加财务管理成本，但也可能降低对美元流动性的依赖风险。

De-dollarization Wave Accelerates

China and Iran's cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz marks an important milestone in the global de-dollarization process. As the world's fourth-largest oil reserve holder, Iran's choice to deepen energy trade priced in Chinese yuan with China is not only a strategy to circumvent U.S. sanctions, but also a direct challenge to dollar hegemony since the Bretton Woods system.

The Strait of Hormuz controls approximately 20% of global oil transportation. The strategic value of this geographic location gives the China-Iran cooperation significance beyond bilateral relations. As more energy transactions bypass the dollar system, the traditional petrodollar recycling mechanism will face fundamental disruption.

Direct Impact on Investors

First, exchange rate volatility risks increase significantly. The weakening of the dollar's position may lead to its depreciation relative to other major currencies. Investors holding dollar assets need to reassess their currency hedging strategies. Meanwhile, the acceleration of the yuan's internationalization may create new investment opportunities for yuan-denominated assets.

Second, the pricing mechanism for commodities faces restructuring. Energy products such as oil and natural gas, traditionally priced in dollars, if increasingly settled in other currencies, will change the price discovery mechanism for these commodities and impact related futures and derivatives markets.

Reshaping the Global Financial System

The de-dollarization trend will promote the formation of a multipolar currency system. Besides the yuan, the use of euros, rubles, and other currencies in international trade is also increasing. This change will make the global financial system more complex, but potentially more stable, as risks are no longer excessively concentrated in a single currency.

For multinational enterprises, this means the need to establish a more diversified currency management system. Companies may need to maintain cash reserves in multiple currencies and establish more sophisticated exchange rate risk management mechanisms. This will increase financial management costs but may also reduce dependency risks on dollar liquidity.
