伊朗中国联手挑战美元霸权

Iran and China Join Forces to Challenge US Dollar Hegemony

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月15日
伊朗和中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过石油贸易去美元化等方式挑战美元霸权地位。这一趋势将对全球金融体系、投资组合和个人资产配置产生深远影响，投资者需关注货币多元化趋势下的机遇与风险。
Iran and China are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, challenging the long-standing dominance of the US dollar through methods such as de-dollarization in oil trade. This trend will have profound impacts on the global financial system, investment portfolios, and personal asset allocation, requiring investors to pay attention to opportunities and risks amid the diversification of currencies.

去美元化浪潮正在加速

在全球地缘政治紧张局势下，伊朗和中国正在霍尔木兹海峡地区展开深度合作，共同挑战长期以来的美元霸权体系。这一发展不仅是地缘政治的重新洗牌，更是对现有国际金融秩序的直接冲击。

霍尔木兹海峡作为全球石油运输的关键通道，每天有约21%的全球石油通过此地。伊朗和中国在此区域的合作，特别是在能源贸易中绕过美元结算的做法，正在为全球金融体系带来结构性变化。

对投资者的直接影响

这一趋势对投资组合的影响是多维度的。首先，美元指数可能面临长期下行压力。随着更多国家寻求替代货币进行贸易结算，美元作为储备货币的需求将逐步减少。投资者需要重新评估美元资产在投资组合中的权重。

其次，大宗商品价格结构将发生变化。当石油等关键商品不再完全依赖美元定价时，价格发现机制将更加复杂。这为商品投资者带来了新的套利机会，但也增加了汇率风险。

人民币和其他替代货币的地位将显著提升。中国积极推动人民币国际化，通过与伊朗等国的能源贸易合作，人民币正在获得更多国际认可。投资者应考虑增加人民币资产配置，包括中国国债、股票市场投资等。

职场和商业机遇

去美元化趋势为相关行业带来新的就业机会。金融科技领域，特别是跨境支付、数字货币和区块链技术将迎来快速发展。专业人士可以关注央行数字货币(CBDC)、跨境贸易融资、风险管理等细分领域的职业发展机会。

国际贸易从业者需要适应多货币结算环境。传统的美元结算模式正在被人民币、欧元、卢布等多种货币组合替代。掌握多货币风险管理技能将成为核心竞争力。

生活成本和签证政策影响

货币体系变化将影响日常生活成本。美元贬值可能推高进口商品价格，特别是能源和大宗商品。消费者需要为潜在的通胀做好准备，考虑投资抗通胀资产如房地产、贵金属等。

各国签证和移民政策也可能受到影响。随着经济实力对比的变化，一些新兴经济体的护照含金量将提升，而传统发达国家的相对吸引力可能下降。考虑海外投资移民的人群需要重新评估目标国家的长期价值。

风险管控策略

面对这一历史性变化，个人和机构都需要采取相应的风险管控措施。资产配置应更加多元化，不能过度依赖单一货币或市场。建议将投资组合分散到不同货币区域，包括美元、人民币、欧元等主要货币资产。

关注地缘政治风险的演变。伊朗和中国的合作可能引发美国更强烈的反制措施，包括新的制裁政策。投资者需要密切跟踪相关政策动向，及时调整投资策略。

这一变化标志着后布雷顿森林体系时代的真正到来。虽然美元霸权不会在短期内完全消失，但多极化货币体系已经开始形成。对于普通投资者而言，这既是挑战也是机遇，关键在于及时适应并抓住变化中的投资机会。

The De-Dollarization Wave is Accelerating

Amidst global geopolitical tensions, Iran and China are deepening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, jointly challenging the long-standing US dollar hegemony system. This development is not only a reshuffling of geopolitics, but a direct impact on the existing international financial order.

As a critical global oil transportation corridor, approximately 21% of the world's daily oil passes through the Strait of Hormuz. The cooperation between Iran and China in this region, particularly their practice of bypassing US dollar settlements in energy trade, is bringing structural changes to the global financial system.

Direct Impact on Investors

This trend has multi-dimensional impacts on investment portfolios. First, the US dollar index may face long-term downward pressure. As more countries seek alternative currencies for trade settlement, the demand for the US dollar as a reserve currency will gradually decrease. Investors need to reassess the weight of US dollar assets in their portfolios.

Second, commodity price structures will undergo changes. When key commodities like oil no longer rely entirely on US dollar pricing, price discovery mechanisms will become more complex. This brings new arbitrage opportunities for commodity investors, but also increases foreign exchange risks.

The status of the Chinese yuan and other alternative currencies will significantly rise. China is actively promoting the internationalization of the yuan, and through energy trade cooperation with Iran and other countries, the yuan is gaining greater international recognition. Investors should consider increasing yuan asset allocation, including Chinese government bonds and stock market investments.

Workplace and Business Opportunities

The de-dollarization trend brings new employment opportunities to related industries. The fintech sector, particularly cross-border payments, digital currencies, and blockchain technology will experience rapid development. Professionals can focus on career opportunities in subdivisions such as central bank digital currencies (CBDC), cross-border trade financing, and risk management.

International trade professionals need to adapt to multi-currency settlement environments. The traditional US dollar settlement model is being replaced by combinations of multiple currencies including the yuan, euro, and ruble. Mastering multi-currency risk management skills will become core competitiveness.

Impact on Living Costs and Visa Policies

Changes in the currency system will affect daily living costs. US dollar depreciation may drive up import commodity prices, particularly energy and commodities. Consumers need to prepare for potential inflation and consider investing in inflation-resistant assets such as real estate and precious metals.

Countries' visa and immigration policies may also be affected. As economic power comparisons change, the value of passports from some emerging economies will increase, while the relative attractiveness of traditional developed countries may decline. Those considering overseas investment immigration need to reassess the long-term value of target countries.

Risk Management Strategies

Facing this historic change, both individuals and institutions need to adopt corresponding risk management measures. Asset allocation should be more diversified and not overly dependent on a single currency or market. It is recommended to distribute investment portfolios across different currency regions, including major currency assets such as the US dollar, Chinese yuan, and euro.

Monitor the evolution of geopolitical risks. Cooperation between Iran and China may trigger stronger countermeasures from the United States, including new sanction policies. Investors need to closely track relevant policy developments and adjust investment strategies in a timely manner.

This change marks the true arrival of the post-Bretton Woods system era. While US dollar hegemony will not completely disappear in the short term, a multipolar currency system is already beginning to take shape. For ordinary investors, this is both a challenge and an opportunity; the key is to adapt in time and seize investment opportunities amid the changes.
