伊朗中国联手挑战美元霸权

Iran and China Join Forces to Challenge Dollar Hegemony

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月08日
在战略要地霍尔木兹海峡，伊朗与中国正通过能源贸易绕过美元结算体系，这一举动可能重塑全球金融格局。对普通人而言，这意味着汇率波动、投资组合调整、以及全球贸易成本变化的连锁反应即将到来。
At the strategic Strait of Hormuz, Iran and China are bypassing dollar settlement systems through energy trade, a move that could reshape the global financial landscape. For ordinary people, this means chain reactions of currency fluctuations, investment portfolio adjustments, and changes in global trade costs are coming.

霍尔木兹海峡的金融革命

全球约40%的海运石油贸易都要经过霍尔木兹海峡这条狭窄水道。如今，这里不仅是能源运输的咽喉，更成为挑战美元霸权的前沿阵地。伊朗与中国正在通过直接的本币结算，绕过传统的美元主导的国际支付体系。

这不是简单的双边贸易安排，而是对战后国际金融秩序的直接挑战。自1944年布雷顿森林体系建立以来，美元一直是国际储备货币和大宗商品贸易的主要结算货币。

对你的投资组合意味着什么

首先是汇率影响。美元霸权的松动将直接影响汇率稳定性。如果更多国家效仿伊朗和中国的做法，美元需求将下降，可能导致美元走弱。对于持有美元资产的投资者，这意味着资产价值的潜在缩水。

相反，人民币和其他替代货币可能获得更多国际认可。投资者需要考虑货币多元化配置，不再将所有鸡蛋放在美元这一个篮子里。黄金、比特币等传统避险资产也可能受益于这种不确定性。

能源股票也将面临重新定价。传统上依赖美元结算的能源公司可能面临成本上升，而那些早已布局多币种结算的公司则可能获得竞争优势。

工作和生活成本的变化

对于普通工薪族，最直接的影响体现在生活成本上。石油价格的任何波动都会传导至汽油、电费、以及各种商品的运输成本。如果美元结算体系受到冲击，短期内可能加剧价格波动。

从事国际贸易、金融服务、或跨国公司工作的人员需要特别关注。传统上依赖SWIFT系统和美元结算的业务模式可能面临调整。掌握人民币、欧元等多种货币操作技能将变得更加重要。

签证和国际流动的考量

地缘政治格局的变化也会影响国际人员流动。美国可能对参与去美元化的国家采取更严格的金融制裁，这可能间接影响这些国家公民的签证申请和国际旅行。

同时，中国作为这一进程的重要参与者，其国际地位的提升可能让中国护照的含金量进一步提高，为中国公民的国际流动创造更多便利。

如何应对这一变化

对个人而言，最重要的是保持资产配置的灵活性。不要过度依赖单一货币或市场，适当配置不同地区、不同货币计价的资产。

关注政策动向同样重要。各国央行的货币政策、国际制裁措施、以及新的贸易协定都可能带来投资机会或风险。

对于企业来说，提前布局多元化的结算体系、培养处理多种货币的能力将成为竞争优势。那些仍然完全依赖美元体系的企业可能面临更大的不确定性。

长远影响展望

这一变化的影响将是渐进而深远的。短期内，我们可能看到更多的市场波动和不确定性。但长期而言，一个更加多极化的国际货币体系可能提供更多选择和更大的稳定性。

重要的是认识到，这不仅仅是两个国家的贸易安排，而是国际秩序重构的一个缩影。适应这种变化，而非抗拒它，才是明智的选择。

Financial Revolution at the Strait of Hormuz

Approximately 40% of global maritime oil trade passes through this narrow waterway of the Strait of Hormuz. Today, it is not only a chokepoint for energy transportation, but has become the frontline for challenging dollar hegemony. Iran and China are bypassing the traditional dollar-dominated international payment system through direct settlement in their own currencies.

This is not a simple bilateral trade arrangement, but a direct challenge to the post-war international financial order. Since the establishment of the Bretton Woods system in 1944, the US dollar has been the primary reserve currency and settlement currency for commodity trade.

What This Means for Your Investment Portfolio

First, there is the currency impact. A loosening of dollar hegemony will directly affect exchange rate stability. If more countries follow Iran and China's approach, dollar demand will decline, potentially leading to a weaker dollar. For investors holding dollar-denominated assets, this means potential shrinkage in asset value.

Conversely, the Chinese yuan and other alternative currencies may gain more international recognition. Investors need to consider diversified currency allocation, no longer putting all eggs in the dollar basket. Traditional safe-haven assets like gold and bitcoin may also benefit from this uncertainty.

Energy stocks will also face repricing. Companies traditionally reliant on dollar settlements may face rising costs, while those that have already deployed multi-currency settlement may gain competitive advantages.

Changes in Work and Living Costs

For ordinary wage earners, the most direct impact is on living costs. Any fluctuations in oil prices will be transmitted to gasoline, electricity, and transportation costs of various commodities. If the dollar settlement system is disrupted, short-term price volatility may intensify.

Those engaged in international trade, financial services, or working for multinational corporations need to pay special attention. Business models traditionally reliant on the SWIFT system and dollar settlement may face adjustments. Mastering operational skills in multiple currencies such as the yuan and euro will become increasingly important.

Considerations for Visas and International Mobility

Changes in geopolitical landscape will also affect international personnel mobility. The United States may impose stricter financial sanctions on countries participating in de-dollarization, which could indirectly affect visa applications and international travel for citizens of these countries.

Meanwhile, China, as an important participant in this process, may see its international standing elevated, making the Chinese passport more valuable and creating more convenience for Chinese citizens' international mobility.

How to Adapt to This Change

For individuals, the most important thing is to maintain flexibility in asset allocation. Do not over-rely on a single currency or market; appropriately allocate assets priced in different regions and different currencies.

Paying attention to policy developments is equally important. Central bank monetary policies, international sanctions measures, and new trade agreements may all bring investment opportunities or risks.

For enterprises, laying out diversified settlement systems in advance and developing capabilities to handle multiple currencies will become competitive advantages. Companies still completely dependent on the dollar system may face greater uncertainty.

Long-term Outlook

The impact of this change will be gradual and far-reaching. In the short term, we may see more market volatility and uncertainty. However, in the long term, a more multipolar international monetary system may offer more choices and greater stability.

The key is to recognize that this is not merely a trade arrangement between two countries, but a microcosm of international order restructuring. Adapting to this change, rather than resisting it, is the wise choice.
