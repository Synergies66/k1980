伊朗战争升级推高房贷利率，全球房市面临冲击

Iran War Escalation Drives Up Mortgage Rates, Global Housing Market Faces Impact

K1980 深度 · 2026年03月30日
地缘政治紧张局势导致美国房贷利率攀升，购房需求急剧下降。投资者避险情绪升温，资金流向国债等安全资产。房地产市场流动性收紧，对建筑、金融等相关行业产生连锁反应。海外置业和移民计划或将受到影响，建议投资者重新评估资产配置策略。
Geopolitical tensions have caused US mortgage rates to surge, leading to a sharp decline in home purchase demand. Rising risk-aversion sentiment among investors has redirected capital flows toward safe-haven assets like government bonds. Real estate market liquidity is tightening, creating chain reactions across construction and financial sectors. Overseas property purchases and immigration plans may be affected, with investors advised to reassess their asset allocation strategies.

地缘冲突引发金融市场震荡

伊朗局势升级正在对全球金融市场产生深远影响，其中房地产市场首当其冲。美国30年期固定房贷利率已从年初的6.8%攀升至7.2%以上，创近20年新高。这一变化源于投资者对地缘政治风险的担忧，大量资金涌入美国国债等避险资产，推高了长期利率水平。

房贷利率每上升0.1个百分点，就会使约20万潜在购房者退出市场。目前的利率水平已使购房成本比去年同期增加约15%，对首次购房者形成巨大压力。

房地产市场需求急剧萎缩

最新数据显示，美国新房销售量环比下降8.7%，二手房市场同样低迷。房地产经纪人协会报告称，看房量较上月减少近三成，购房合同签约数创2020年疫情以来新低。

高利率环境下，月供压力显著增加。以50万美元房屋为例，按当前利率计算的月供比年初增加约400美元。这迫使许多潜在买家推迟购房计划，转向租房市场，进一步推高了租金水平。

建筑商信心指数也跌至近两年低点，新开工项目数量明显减少。大型建筑企业开始调整策略，将重点转向租赁住房和经济适用房项目。

投资策略面临重大调整

房地产投资信托基金(REITs)遭受重创，住宅类REITs本月跌幅超过12%。机构投资者开始重新评估房地产资产配置，部分基金已减持地产相关股票。

对于个人投资者而言，当前环境提供了不同的机会和挑战。现金充裕的投资者可能在未来几个月找到更好的购房时机，特别是在房价开始回调的地区。但杠杆投资者需要格外谨慎，高利率环境将显著增加投资成本。

海外置业和移民计划受阻

地缘政治紧张局势也影响了跨境房地产投资。美国EB-5投资移民项目的房地产类项目融资成本上升，部分项目被迫推迟。欧洲黄金签证项目同样面临挑战，投资门槛实际上有所提高。

中国投资者的海外置业活动明显放缓，不仅因为成本上升，也因为汇率波动和资金流动限制。澳大利亚、加拿大等热门目的地的中国买家询盘量下降超过30%。

未来展望与应对建议

分析师预计，如果地缘紧张局势持续，房贷利率可能进一步上升至7.5%-8%区间。这将对经济产生更广泛影响，包括消费支出减少、就业市场降温等。

对于普通购房者，建议在当前环境下保持谨慎：首先评估个人财务状况，确保有足够的现金流应对高月供；其次考虑浮动利率产品，以备未来利率下降时转换；最后密切关注地方政策，一些州政府可能推出购房补贴计划。

投资者应多元化资产配置，避免过度集中于房地产。可考虑投资基础设施、能源等在通胀环境下表现较好的资产类别。同时，保持一定的现金储备，以便在市场出现更好机会时快速行动。

长期来看，住房需求的基本面仍然稳固，特别是在人口增长较快的地区。当前的市场调整可能为理性投资者创造机会，关键是把握好时机和节奏。

Geopolitical Conflicts Trigger Financial Market Volatility

Escalating tensions in Iran are having profound effects on global financial markets, with the real estate sector bearing the brunt. US 30-year fixed mortgage rates have climbed from 6.8% at the beginning of the year to above 7.2%, reaching a 20-year high. This shift stems from investor concerns about geopolitical risks, with massive capital inflows into safe-haven assets like US Treasury bonds, pushing long-term interest rates upward.

For every 0.1 percentage point increase in mortgage rates, approximately 200,000 potential homebuyers are priced out of the market. Current rate levels have increased home purchase costs by roughly 15% compared to last year, creating enormous pressure on first-time buyers.

Real Estate Market Demand Collapses Sharply

Latest data shows US new home sales declined 8.7% month-over-month, with the secondhand housing market equally sluggish. The National Association of Realtors reports that showings have decreased nearly 30% from the previous month, with home purchase contracts at their lowest levels since the 2020 pandemic.

In the high-interest environment, monthly mortgage payments have increased significantly. For a $500,000 home, the monthly payment calculated at current rates is about $400 higher than at the start of the year. This has forced many potential buyers to delay purchase plans and turn to the rental market, further driving up rents.

Builder confidence indices have plummeted to near two-year lows, with new construction starts declining noticeably. Major construction firms are adjusting strategies, shifting focus toward rental housing and affordable housing projects.

Investment Strategies Face Major Adjustments

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) have been hit hard, with residential REITs dropping over 12% this month. Institutional investors are reassessing real estate asset allocations, with some funds already reducing real estate-related holdings.

For individual investors, the current environment presents both opportunities and challenges. Cash-rich investors may find better opportunities to purchase homes in the coming months, particularly in regions where prices begin to decline. However, leveraged investors must exercise extreme caution, as high interest rates will significantly increase investment costs.

Overseas Property Purchases and Immigration Plans Blocked

Geopolitical tensions are also affecting cross-border real estate investment. Financing costs for real estate projects under the US EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program have risen, with some projects forced to postpone. European golden visa programs face similar challenges, with effective investment thresholds effectively raised.

Overseas property acquisition activities by Chinese investors have notably slowed, not only due to rising costs but also due to currency fluctuations and capital flow restrictions. Inquiries from Chinese buyers in popular destinations like Australia and Canada have declined by over 30%.

Future Outlook and Recommendations

Analysts predict that if geopolitical tensions persist, mortgage rates could further rise to the 7.5%-8% range. This would have broader economic impacts, including reduced consumer spending and cooling labor markets.

For ordinary homebuyers, it's recommended to exercise caution in the current environment: first, assess personal financial situations and ensure sufficient cash flow to handle higher monthly payments; second, consider variable-rate mortgage products to enable switching if rates decline in the future; third, closely monitor local policies, as some state governments may introduce homebuying subsidy programs.

Investors should diversify asset allocations and avoid over-concentration in real estate. Consider investing in infrastructure, energy, and other asset classes that perform well in inflationary environments. Simultaneously, maintain adequate cash reserves to act quickly when better market opportunities emerge.

Long-term fundamentals of housing demand remain solid, especially in regions with rapid population growth. Current market corrections may create opportunities for rational investors; the key is seizing the right timing and pace.
