中伊合作冲击美元霸权

China-Iran Cooperation Challenges Dollar Hegemony

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月11日
伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，挑战美元在全球贸易中的主导地位。这一趋势可能重塑国际金融格局，影响全球投资者的资产配置策略，并对依赖美元体系的企业和个人产生深远影响。去美元化进程正在加速，投资者需重新审视风险管理和多元化投资组合。
Iran and China are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, challenging the dominance of the dollar in global trade. This trend may reshape the international financial landscape, impact global investors' asset allocation strategies, and have profound effects on enterprises and individuals reliant on the dollar system. The de-dollarization process is accelerating, requiring investors to reassess risk management and diversify investment portfolios.

去美元化浪潮兴起

在霍尔木兹海峡这一全球石油运输要道，伊朗与中国正在悄然改写国际贸易规则。两国绕过美元结算体系，采用人民币和本币进行石油贸易，这一举措正在撼动美元在国际储备货币中的垄断地位。

对于普通投资者而言，这意味着全球金融格局正在发生根本性变化。传统的美元避险资产地位可能面临挑战，多元化货币储备成为新趋势。

对个人投资组合的影响

这一变化对投资者的资产配置策略产生直接影响。首先，美元资产的相对吸引力可能下降，投资者应考虑增加人民币、欧元等其他货币计价资产的比重。其次，能源股票、黄金等传统避险资产可能迎来新的投资机会。

对于持有美元债券的投资者，需要密切关注美元流动性变化。随着更多国家加入去美元化进程，美元需求减少可能推高通胀，影响美联储货币政策，进而影响债券收益率。

企业运营成本重构

跨国企业面临汇率风险管理的新挑战。传统上依赖美元结算的企业需要重新评估外汇对冲策略。特别是与中东、亚洲有业务往来的公司，可能需要建立多币种结算体系，增加财务管理复杂度。

能源密集型企业可能从中受益。绕过美元的石油贸易可能降低交易成本，减少汇率波动风险，最终降低能源采购成本。

就业市场新机遇

金融服务业将面临结构性调整。传统美元交易相关岗位可能减少，但多币种金融产品设计、风险管理等新岗位需求增加。掌握人民币、中东货币知识的专业人才将更受欢迎。

国际贸易领域对精通多语言、了解不同支付体系的人才需求上升。特别是熟悉中国金融科技、数字货币的专业人士将获得更多职业机会。

签证和移民考量

对于考虑海外投资移民的人群，货币格局变化提供了新的思考角度。传统的美元资产配置策略需要调整，考虑分散投资到人民币区域经济体。

新加坡、香港等国际金融中心可能因多币种交易需求增加而更具吸引力。这些地区的投资移民项目可能迎来新的发展机遇。

风险管理新策略

个人和企业都需要建立新的风险管理框架。传统的美元计价风险模型可能不再适用，需要考虑多货币波动的复杂影响。

建议投资者关注：一是增加贵金属配置，作为货币体系转换期的避险工具；二是关注受益于去美元化的科技公司，特别是支付清算技术企业；三是考虑投资多元化货币基金，分散单一货币风险。

未来展望

中伊在霍尔木兹海峡的合作只是全球去美元化进程的一个缩影。随着更多国家加入这一趋势，国际金融体系将更加多极化。这既带来挑战，也创造了新的投资和职业机会。

适应这一变化的关键在于保持开放心态，及时调整投资策略，关注新兴市场货币和相关资产的投资机会。

The Rise of De-Dollarization Wave

In the Strait of Hormuz, a critical global oil transportation route, Iran and China are quietly rewriting international trade rules. The two countries are bypassing the dollar settlement system and conducting oil trade using the yuan and local currencies, a move that is shaking the dollar's monopoly position as the international reserve currency.

For ordinary investors, this signifies a fundamental shift in the global financial landscape. The traditional safe-haven status of dollar assets may face challenges, and diversified currency reserves are becoming the new trend.

Impact on Personal Investment Portfolios

This change directly affects investors' asset allocation strategies. First, the relative attractiveness of dollar assets may decline, and investors should consider increasing the proportion of assets denominated in other currencies such as the yuan and euro. Second, energy stocks, gold, and other traditional safe-haven assets may face new investment opportunities.

For investors holding dollar bonds, it is necessary to closely monitor changes in dollar liquidity. As more countries join the de-dollarization process, reduced dollar demand may push up inflation, affecting Federal Reserve monetary policy, and subsequently impacting bond yields.

Corporate Operating Cost Restructuring

Multinational enterprises face new challenges in foreign exchange risk management. Companies traditionally reliant on dollar settlement need to reassess their forex hedging strategies. Particularly for companies with business operations in the Middle East and Asia, there may be a need to establish multi-currency settlement systems, increasing financial management complexity.

Energy-intensive enterprises may benefit from this. Oil trading that bypasses the dollar may reduce transaction costs, minimize currency fluctuation risks, and ultimately lower energy procurement costs.

New Opportunities in the Job Market

The financial services industry will face structural adjustments. Traditional dollar trading-related positions may decline, but demand for multi-currency financial product design and risk management roles will increase. Professionals with knowledge of the yuan and Middle Eastern currencies will become more sought-after.

The international trade sector will see increased demand for talent proficient in multiple languages and familiar with different payment systems. Professionals with expertise in Chinese fintech and digital currencies will gain more career opportunities.

Visa and Immigration Considerations

For those considering overseas investment immigration, currency landscape changes provide new perspectives for thought. Traditional dollar asset allocation strategies need adjustment, considering diversified investments in the yuan-denominated economic zones.

Financial centers like Singapore and Hong Kong may become more attractive due to increased multi-currency trading demand. Investment immigration projects in these regions may face new development opportunities.

New Risk Management Strategies

Both individuals and enterprises need to establish new risk management frameworks. Traditional dollar-denominated risk models may no longer be applicable, requiring consideration of complex impacts from multi-currency fluctuations.

Investors are advised to focus on: first, increasing precious metals allocation as a safe-haven tool during the currency system transition; second, paying attention to technology companies that benefit from de-dollarization, particularly payment settlement technology enterprises; third, considering investments in diversified currency funds to spread single-currency risk.

Future Outlook

China-Iran cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz is merely a microcosm of the global de-dollarization process. As more countries join this trend, the international financial system will become more multipolar. This brings both challenges and creates new investment and career opportunities.

The key to adapting to this change lies in maintaining an open mind, timely adjusting investment strategies, and focusing on emerging market currencies and related asset investment opportunities.
