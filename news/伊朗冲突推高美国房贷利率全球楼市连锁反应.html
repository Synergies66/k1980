Iran Conflict Pushes Up US Mortgage Rates, Triggering Global Real Estate Chain Reactions

K1980 深度 · 2026年03月27日
地缘政治紧张局势导致美国房贷利率再次攀升，投资者避险情绪推动债券市场波动。这一变化不仅影响美国购房者和房地产投资者，也将通过资本流动、汇率变化等渠道影响全球房地产市场，对海外投资者、移民购房需求产生深远影响。
Geopolitical tensions have caused US mortgage rates to rise again, with investors' risk-aversion sentiment driving volatility in bond markets. This change not only affects American homebuyers and real estate investors, but will also impact the global real estate market through capital flows, exchange rate changes, and other channels, producing profound effects on overseas investors and immigrant homebuying demand.

地缘冲突如何影响房贷利率

伊朗地区紧张局势升级，金融市场立即做出反应。投资者纷纷转向避险资产，美国国债需求激增，但同时通胀担忧也在上升。油价波动、供应链风险重新浮现，美联储货币政策预期发生微妙变化，这些因素综合作用下，房贷利率出现新一轮上涨。

历史数据显示，每当中东出现重大地缘政治事件，美国房贷利率往往在短期内出现0.1-0.3个百分点的波动。当前30年期固定房贷利率已从年初的6.8%上升至7.2%附近，对购房者构成实质性压力。

对美国房地产市场的直接冲击

房贷利率上升直接影响购房能力。以40万美元房屋为例，利率从6.8%上升至7.2%，月供增加约120美元，30年总成本增加4.3万美元。这迫使许多潜在买家推迟购房计划，特别是首次购房者群体。

房地产开发商面临双重压力：建设成本因油价上涨而增加，同时需求下降导致去库存周期延长。上市房企股价普遍下跌5-8%，反映市场对行业前景的担忧。租赁市场可能因购房需求转移而升温，为投资者带来新机遇。

How Geopolitical Conflicts Affect Mortgage Rates

Escalating tensions in the Iran region have triggered immediate reactions in financial markets. Investors are rushing toward safe-haven assets, surging demand for US Treasury bonds, while inflation concerns are also rising. Oil price volatility, supply chain risks re-emerging, and subtle shifts in Federal Reserve monetary policy expectations—these factors combined have led to a new round of mortgage rate increases.

Historical data shows that whenever major geopolitical events occur in the Middle East, US mortgage rates typically experience fluctuations of 0.1-0.3 percentage points in the short term. The current 30-year fixed mortgage rate has risen from 6.8% at the beginning of the year to around 7.2%, creating substantial pressure on homebuyers.

Direct Impact on the US Real Estate Market

Rising mortgage rates directly affect purchasing power. For a $400,000 house, a rate increase from 6.8% to 7.2% raises monthly payments by approximately $120, increasing the 30-year total cost by $43,000. This forces many potential buyers to postpone their homebuying plans, particularly first-time homebuyers.

Real estate developers face dual pressures: construction costs rise due to oil price increases, while declining demand extends the inventory liquidation cycle. Listed real estate company stocks have generally fallen 5-8%, reflecting market concerns about industry prospects. The rental market may heat up due to shifted homebuying demand, bringing new opportunities for investors.
