中伊霍尔木兹海峡去美元化冲击全球金融

China and Iran's De-dollarization in the Strait of Hormuz Shakes Global Finance

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月17日
伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡推进去美元化交易，挑战美元霸权地位。此举可能重塑全球贸易结算体系，影响美元汇率、大宗商品价格和投资组合配置。对普通投资者而言，需关注能源价格波动、新兴市场货币走势，以及数字货币和黄金等避险资产的投资机会。
Iran and China are advancing de-dollarization transactions in the Strait of Hormuz, challenging the hegemony of the US dollar. This move could reshape the global trade settlement system and impact dollar exchange rates, commodity prices, and investment portfolio allocation. For ordinary investors, attention should be paid to energy price volatility, emerging market currency trends, and investment opportunities in digital currencies and safe-haven assets like gold.

去美元化浪潮加速

伊朗与中国在全球石油贸易的咽喉要道——霍尔木兹海峡推进去美元化进程，标志着对美元国际储备货币地位的直接挑战。这一战略性举措不仅是地缘政治博弈的体现，更可能引发全球金融体系的深刻变革。

霍尔木兹海峡控制着全球约20%的石油运输，伊朗与中国在此区域推行本币结算，实际上是在构建绕过美元的平行金融体系。这种做法正逐渐得到俄罗斯、委内瑞拉等国响应，形成去美元化联盟。

对投资组合的直接冲击

对于投资者而言，这一趋势将带来多重影响。首先是汇率层面，美元指数可能面临长期下行压力。当越来越多的国际贸易脱离美元结算，对美元的需求将相应减少，这可能导致美元相对其他主要货币贬值。

持有美元资产的投资者需要重新评估风险敞口。传统的美元债券、美股等资产可能面临估值调整压力。相反，人民币、黄金等资产可能获得更多关注。特别是黄金，作为传统的价值储存工具，在去美元化进程中往往表现强劲。

能源市场重新洗牌

能源投资是受影响最直接的领域。中伊能源合作的深化可能改变全球石油定价机制。目前国际油价主要以美元计价，但如果更多交易转向人民币或其他货币，将削弱美国通过金融制裁控制能源贸易的能力。

对于能源股投资者，需要关注哪些公司能够适应新的结算体系。那些在中东、亚洲有深度布局，且具备多货币结算能力的能源企业可能获得竞争优势。同时，新能源领域可能因地缘政治风险而获得额外关注。

新兴市场货币机会显现

去美元化为新兴市场货币创造了历史性机会。人民币国际化进程将明显加速，"一带一路"沿线国家使用人民币结算的意愿可能显著提升。这为人民币计价的债券、存款等金融产品创造了需求。

对于跨国工作的专业人士，需要考虑薪酬货币的选择。在去美元化趋势下，持有多元化货币资产变得更加重要。特别是在中东、亚洲工作的人员，可以考虑增加人民币、当地货币的配置比例。

数字货币异军突起

值得注意的是，去美元化进程可能催化央行数字货币(CBDC)的发展。中国的数字人民币、伊朗正在开发的数字里亚尔等，可能成为绕过传统美元主导的SWIFT系统的重要工具。

对于科技投资者，区块链、数字支付、跨境结算等相关技术领域值得重点关注。那些为央行数字货币提供技术支持的公司可能迎来巨大发展机遇。

风险管控策略调整

面对这一变局，个人和机构都需要调整风险管控策略。多元化投资变得更加重要，不能过度依赖单一货币或市场。建议将资产分散配置到不同货币、不同地区的市场中。

对于有海外投资需求的人士，需要密切关注汇率走势和资本管制政策变化。同时，保持一定比例的实物资产配置，如房地产、贵金属等，以对冲货币贬值风险。

这场去美元化变革正在重塑全球金融格局，虽然过程可能充满波折，但趋势已经形成。明智的投资者应该提前布局，在变革中寻找机遇。

De-dollarization Wave Accelerates

Iran and China are advancing de-dollarization initiatives in the Strait of Hormuz, the critical chokepoint of global oil trade, marking a direct challenge to the US dollar's status as the international reserve currency. This strategic move is not only a manifestation of geopolitical competition but could also trigger profound transformations in the global financial system.

The Strait of Hormuz controls approximately 20% of global oil shipments. Iran and China's implementation of local currency settlement in this region essentially constructs a parallel financial system that bypasses the dollar. This practice is gradually gaining responses from countries like Russia and Venezuela, forming a de-dollarization alliance.

Direct Impact on Investment Portfolios

For investors, this trend will bring multiple impacts. First, at the exchange rate level, the US dollar index may face long-term downward pressure. As more international trade moves away from dollar settlement, demand for dollars will correspondingly decrease, potentially causing the dollar to depreciate against other major currencies.

Investors holding dollar-denominated assets need to reassess their risk exposure. Traditional assets such as dollar bonds and US stocks may face valuation adjustment pressures. Conversely, assets like the yuan and gold may attract more attention. Gold in particular, as a traditional store of value, typically performs strongly during de-dollarization processes.

Energy Market Reshuffled

Energy investment is the field most directly affected. The deepening of China-Iran energy cooperation could change global oil pricing mechanisms. Currently, international oil prices are primarily denominated in dollars, but if more transactions shift to the yuan or other currencies, it will weaken the US ability to control energy trade through financial sanctions.

For energy stock investors, attention should be paid to which companies can adapt to the new settlement system. Energy enterprises with deep deployment in the Middle East and Asia and multi-currency settlement capabilities may gain competitive advantages. Meanwhile, the renewable energy sector may attract additional attention due to geopolitical risks.

Emerging Market Currency Opportunities Emerge

De-dollarization creates a historic opportunity for emerging market currencies. The internationalization process of the yuan will notably accelerate, and the willingness of "Belt and Road" countries to use the yuan for settlement may significantly increase. This creates demand for yuan-denominated financial products such as bonds and deposits.

For multinational professionals, the choice of compensation currency needs consideration. In the context of de-dollarization trends, holding diversified currency assets becomes increasingly important. Particularly for those working in the Middle East and Asia, considering an increase in yuan and local currency allocation is advisable.

Digital Currencies Rise Unexpectedly

Notably, the de-dollarization process may catalyze the development of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs). China's Digital Yuan, Iran's Digital Rial under development, and others may become important tools to bypass the traditional dollar-dominated SWIFT system.

For technology investors, blockchain, digital payments, and cross-border settlement technologies are worth focusing on. Companies providing technical support for central bank digital currencies may face enormous development opportunities.

Risk Management Strategy Adjustments

Facing this situation, both individuals and institutions need to adjust risk management strategies. Diversification becomes increasingly important; excessive reliance on a single currency or market should be avoided. It is recommended to distribute assets across different currencies and different regional markets.

For those with overseas investment needs, close attention should be paid to exchange rate movements and changes in capital control policies. Simultaneously, maintaining a certain proportion of tangible asset allocations, such as real estate and precious metals, helps hedge against currency depreciation risks.

This de-dollarization transformation is reshaping the global financial landscape. Although the process may be turbulent, the trend has been established. Prudent investors should position themselves early and seize opportunities amid the transformation.
