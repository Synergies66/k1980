伊朗中国霍尔木兹挑战美元霸权

Iran, China Challenge Dollar Hegemony in Hormuz Strait

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月12日
伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强能源贸易合作，推动人民币结算，挑战美元在国际石油贸易中的主导地位。此举可能重塑全球金融格局，影响美元汇率、石油价格和投资市场，对持有美元资产的投资者和跨国企业带来汇率风险，同时为人民币国际化和新兴市场货币创造机遇。
Iran and China are strengthening energy trade cooperation in the Hormuz Strait region and promoting settlement in yuan, challenging the dollar's dominance in international oil trade. This move could reshape the global financial landscape, affecting dollar exchange rates, oil prices, and investment markets, bringing currency risks to dollar asset holders and multinational enterprises, while creating opportunities for yuan internationalization and emerging market currencies.

地缘政治新格局正在形成

伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡这一全球石油运输咽喉要道的合作，标志着国际金融秩序正面临深刻变革。霍尔木兹海峡承载着全球约20%的石油运输量，两国在此区域推动去美元化，具有重要的象征意义和实际影响。

中伊两国正通过能源贸易绕开美元结算体系，这不仅是对美国制裁的回应，更是对布雷顿森林体系以来美元霸权的直接挑战。伊朗丰富的石油天然气资源与中国庞大的能源需求形成天然互补，为替代性货币结算体系提供了坚实基础。

对投资者的直接影响

美元资产持有者需要重新评估投资组合风险。随着更多国家寻求美元替代方案，美元指数可能面临长期下行压力。这对美国国债、美元计价的大宗商品基金以及美股投资者都构成挑战。

相反，人民币资产的吸引力正在上升。中国债券市场、A股以及人民币计价的商品期货可能获得更多国际资金关注。投资者应考虑适度增加人民币资产配置，对冲美元贬值风险。

石油市场将出现分化。传统的WTI和布伦特原油期货仍以美元计价，但人民币计价的上海原油期货影响力正在扩大。能源类投资者需要关注不同计价货币的价格差异带来的套利机会。

企业运营策略调整

跨国企业特别是能源贸易公司面临货币选择难题。与中东地区有业务往来的企业需要建立多元化的货币结算能力，减少对美元的过度依赖。这可能增加财务管理成本，但能降低制裁风险。

中国企业在"一带一路"沿线国家的项目可能获得更多政策支持。基础设施建设、能源开发、金融服务等领域的中国企业将迎来新机遇。同时，掌握多语言和跨文化管理能力的专业人才需求将大幅增长。

就业市场新趋势

金融行业正在经历技能需求转变。熟悉人民币国际化、数字货币、跨境支付等领域的专业人士将更加抢手。传统美元交易员可能需要学习新的货币对和市场规则。

能源行业的就业重心正向亚洲转移。石油贸易、航运物流、金融服务等相关岗位在中东和亚洲地区增长明显。掌握中文、阿拉伯语等语言技能的从业者将获得更多机会。

签证和移民政策影响

中国与伊朗等中东国家的人员往来正在增加。中国可能进一步放宽对相关国家的签证政策，促进商务和技术交流。对于寻求海外发展机会的专业人士，中东地区可能成为新的热门目的地。

美国对伊朗等国的制裁政策可能影响相关人员的签证申请。有伊朗等被制裁国家工作经历的人员在申请美国签证时可能面临更严格审查。

生活成本变化预期

能源价格波动将直接影响生活成本。短期内，地缘政治紧张可能推高油价，增加交通和取暖费用。但长期来看，能源供应渠道多元化有助于价格稳定。

汇率波动将影响进口商品价格。美元走弱可能推高以美元计价的进口商品成本，但也会降低海外旅游和留学费用。消费者需要根据汇率趋势调整消费计划。

这一变化标志着多极化世界秩序的形成，每个人都需要适应更加复杂但也更加多元化的国际环境。

A New Geopolitical Pattern is Forming

The cooperation between Iran and China in the Hormuz Strait, a critical global oil transportation chokepoint, marks a profound transformation in the international financial order. The Strait carries approximately 20% of global oil shipments, and the two countries' push for de-dollarization in this region carries significant symbolic and practical implications.

China and Iran are bypassing the dollar settlement system through energy trade, which is not only a response to U.S. sanctions but also a direct challenge to dollar hegemony since the Bretton Woods system. Iran's abundant oil and natural gas resources complement China's massive energy demand, providing a solid foundation for alternative currency settlement systems.

Direct Impact on Investors

Dollar asset holders need to reassess portfolio risks. As more countries seek dollar alternatives, the dollar index may face long-term downward pressure. This poses challenges for U.S. Treasury bonds, dollar-denominated commodity funds, and U.S. stock investors.

Conversely, the attractiveness of yuan assets is rising. Chinese bond markets, A-shares, and yuan-denominated commodity futures may attract more international capital. Investors should consider modestly increasing yuan asset allocation to hedge against dollar depreciation risk.

The oil market will experience differentiation. Traditional WTI and Brent crude oil futures remain dollar-priced, but Shanghai crude oil futures priced in yuan are expanding their influence. Energy investors need to monitor arbitrage opportunities created by price differences across various pricing currencies.

Corporate Operations Strategy Adjustments

Multinational enterprises, particularly those in energy trading, face currency selection challenges. Companies with business operations in the Middle East need to establish diversified currency settlement capabilities to reduce excessive reliance on the dollar. This may increase financial management costs but can reduce sanctions risks.

Chinese enterprises' projects along the "Belt and Road" countries may receive more policy support. Chinese companies in infrastructure development, energy exploration, and financial services will face new opportunities. Meanwhile, demand for professionals with multilingual and cross-cultural management skills will surge significantly.

New Employment Market Trends

The financial industry is experiencing changing skill requirements. Professionals familiar with yuan internationalization, digital currencies, and cross-border payments will become increasingly valuable. Traditional dollar traders may need to learn new currency pairs and market rules.

The employment focus in the energy industry is shifting toward Asia. Growth in oil trading, shipping logistics, financial services, and related positions is evident in the Middle East and Asian regions. Professionals proficient in Chinese, Arabic, and other languages will have more opportunities.

Impact on Visa and Immigration Policies

People-to-people exchanges between China and Middle Eastern countries like Iran are increasing. China may further relax visa policies for relevant countries to promote business and technical exchanges. For professionals seeking overseas opportunities, the Middle East may become a new popular destination.

U.S. sanctions policies against Iran and other countries may affect visa applications of relevant personnel. Individuals with work experience in sanctioned countries like Iran may face stricter scrutiny when applying for U.S. visas.

Expected Changes in Living Costs

Energy price fluctuations will directly impact living costs. In the short term, geopolitical tensions may drive up oil prices, increasing transportation and heating expenses. However, in the long run, diversification of energy supply channels will help stabilize prices.

Exchange rate fluctuations will affect import commodity prices. A weakening dollar may increase costs of dollar-denominated imported goods but will also reduce overseas travel and study abroad expenses. Consumers need to adjust consumption plans according to exchange rate trends.

This change marks the formation of a multipolar world order, where everyone needs to adapt to a more complex yet diverse international environment.
