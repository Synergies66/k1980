中伊联手挑战美元霸权，全球金融格局生变

China and Iran Join Forces to Challenge Dollar Hegemony, Global Financial Landscape Shifts

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月11日
伊朗和中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过石油贸易去美元化、数字货币试点等方式挑战美元霸权。这一趋势将影响全球汇率稳定、投资组合配置，并可能推动更多国家寻求美元替代方案，对普通投资者的资产配置和跨境业务产生深远影响。
Iran and China are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region through de-dollarization of oil trade and digital currency pilot programs to challenge dollar hegemony. This trend will impact global exchange rate stability, investment portfolio allocation, and may prompt more countries to seek alternatives to the dollar, with profound implications for ordinary investors' asset allocation and cross-border business operations.

中伊合作的战略意图

霍尔木兹海峡作为全球石油运输的咽喉要道，承载着世界约20%的石油贸易。伊朗和中国在此区域的合作并非偶然，而是对当前美元主导的国际金融体系的直接挑战。伊朗长期受美国制裁困扰，迫切需要绕过美元结算体系；中国则希望减少对美元的依赖，提升人民币国际地位。

双方合作主要体现在石油贸易的去美元化上。中国通过人民币直接购买伊朗石油，避开了传统的美元结算体系。同时，两国还在探索数字货币在大宗商品贸易中的应用，这种创新模式可能成为其他国家效仿的样本。

对全球金融市场的冲击

这种去美元化趋势正在产生连锁反应。首先，美元的全球储备货币地位面临挑战。当越来越多国家寻求美元替代方案时，美元需求可能下降，进而影响美国的货币政策传导效果。

其次，国际油价定价机制可能发生变化。传统上，石油以美元计价，但中伊合作模式可能催生新的定价基准。上海原油期货等以人民币计价的能源合约正获得更多关注，这将重塑全球能源市场格局。

对于投资者而言，这意味着汇率波动可能加剧。美元指数的稳定性可能受到冲击，而人民币、欧元等其他主要货币的重要性将上升。

对普通投资者的实际影响

资产配置调整：投资者需要重新评估货币配置策略。过度依赖美元资产可能面临风险，适当增加其他货币资产配置变得必要。黄金等传统避险资产可能重新受到青睐，因为它们不依赖任何单一货币体系。

股市投资机会：去美元化趋势可能为某些行业带来机遇。数字货币技术公司、跨境支付服务商、以及在新兴市场有布局的能源企业可能受益。同时，过度依赖美元业务的金融机构可能面临挑战。

跨境业务成本：对于有海外业务的个人和企业，汇率风险管理变得更加重要。多元化的结算货币选择可能降低汇率风险，但也增加了操作复杂性。

职业发展考量

金融行业机遇：熟悉多货币体系、数字货币技术、以及新兴市场的金融专业人士将更受欢迎。传统的美元中心化业务模式正在改变，相关技能需求也在演进。

国际贸易领域：了解人民币结算、数字货币应用的贸易专业人士将获得竞争优势。企业对能够处理多元化结算方式的人才需求增加。

签证和移民影响：虽然直接影响有限，但货币格局变化可能影响各国经济实力对比，进而影响移民目的地选择。美国经济霸权地位的相对下降可能使其他发达国家变得更具吸引力。

应对策略建议

面对这一趋势，普通人可以采取以下措施：保持投资组合的货币多元化，不要过度集中在美元资产；关注数字货币和区块链技术发展，这些可能成为未来金融基础设施；提升国际化视野和跨文化沟通能力，适应多极化的世界格局。

中伊在霍尔木兹海峡的合作只是全球金融格局变化的一个缩影。这种变化将是渐进的，但影响深远。对于个人而言，关键是保持敏锐的市场嗅觉，及时调整策略以适应新的国际环境。

Strategic Intentions of China-Iran Cooperation

The Strait of Hormuz, as the lifeline of global oil transportation, handles approximately 20% of the world's oil trade. The cooperation between Iran and China in this region is not coincidental but a direct challenge to the current dollar-dominated international financial system. Iran, long plagued by US sanctions, urgently needs to bypass the dollar settlement system; China seeks to reduce its dependence on the dollar and enhance the international status of the yuan.

The cooperation between the two countries is primarily reflected in the de-dollarization of oil trade. China directly purchases Iranian oil with yuan, bypassing the traditional dollar settlement system. Meanwhile, the two countries are also exploring the application of digital currencies in commodity trading, a model that could serve as a template for other nations to emulate.

Impact on Global Financial Markets

This de-dollarization trend is creating a ripple effect. First, the dollar's position as the global reserve currency faces challenges. As more countries seek alternatives to the dollar, dollar demand may decline, affecting the transmission of US monetary policy.

Second, the international oil pricing mechanism may undergo transformation. Traditionally, oil is priced in dollars, but the China-Iran cooperation model may give rise to new pricing benchmarks. Energy contracts priced in yuan, such as Shanghai crude oil futures, are gaining more attention, which will reshape the global energy market landscape.

For investors, this means exchange rate volatility may intensify. The stability of the dollar index may be undermined, while the importance of other major currencies like the yuan and euro will rise.

Practical Impact on Ordinary Investors

Asset Allocation Adjustments: Investors need to reassess their currency allocation strategy. Over-reliance on dollar assets may face risks, making it necessary to appropriately increase allocations to other currency assets. Traditional safe-haven assets like gold may regain favor, as they do not depend on any single currency system.

Stock Investment Opportunities: The de-dollarization trend may create opportunities for certain industries. Digital currency technology companies, cross-border payment service providers, and energy enterprises with layouts in emerging markets may benefit. Meanwhile, financial institutions overly dependent on dollar business may face challenges.

Cross-Border Business Costs: For individuals and enterprises with overseas operations, exchange rate risk management becomes increasingly important. Diversified settlement currency options may reduce exchange rate risk, but also increase operational complexity.

Career Development Considerations

Financial Industry Opportunities: Financial professionals familiar with multi-currency systems, digital currency technology, and emerging markets will be in higher demand. Traditional dollar-centric business models are changing, and skill requirements are evolving accordingly.

International Trade Sector: Trade professionals knowledgeable about yuan settlement and digital currency applications will gain competitive advantages. Companies are increasing demand for talent capable of handling diversified settlement methods.

Visa and Immigration Impact: While direct impact is limited, changes in the currency landscape may affect the relative economic power of nations, thereby influencing emigration destination choices. The relative decline of US economic hegemony may make other developed countries more attractive.

Recommended Response Strategies

Facing this trend, ordinary people can take the following measures: maintain currency diversification in investment portfolios and avoid over-concentration in dollar assets; pay attention to digital currency and blockchain technology development, which may become the foundation of future financial infrastructure; enhance international perspectives and cross-cultural communication skills to adapt to a multipolar world order.

The cooperation between China and Iran at the Strait of Hormuz is merely a microcosm of changes in the global financial landscape. These changes will be gradual but far-reaching. For individuals, the key is to maintain keen market awareness and adjust strategies in time to adapt to the new international environment.
