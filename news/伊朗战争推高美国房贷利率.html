伊朗战争推高美国房贷利率

Iran Conflict Pushes Up US Mortgage Rates

K1980 深度 · 2026年03月26日
中东冲突升级导致美国房贷利率再次攀升，30年期固定利率已接近7.5%，创近20年新高。地缘政治风险推动避险情绪，债券市场波动加剧，购房者面临更高借贷成本。房地产市场或将进一步降温，影响购房需求和房价走势。投资者需关注利率变化对房地产投资信托基金的冲击。
Middle East tensions escalate, driving US mortgage rates higher, with 30-year fixed rates approaching 7.5%, hitting a 20-year high. Geopolitical risks fuel risk-aversion sentiment and bond market volatility, forcing homebuyers to face increased borrowing costs. The real estate market may cool further, impacting housing demand and price trends. Investors should monitor the impact of rate changes on real estate investment trusts.

地缘冲突引发金融市场连锁反应

伊朗与以色列冲突升级正在美国房地产市场引发连锁反应。投资者对中东地缘政治风险的担忧推动了避险资金流动，但与传统预期不同，资金并未大量流入美国国债，反而因通胀预期和石油价格上涨推高了长期利率。

30年期固定房贷利率已从上月的6.8%跃升至7.5%附近，创下2000年以来的最高水平。这一急剧上升主要源于投资者对通胀重新抬头的担忧，以及美联储可能被迫维持更长时间高利率政策的预期。

购房者承压，市场活动锐减

利率飙升直接冲击了购房者的负担能力。以40万美元房贷为例，利率从6.8%上升到7.5%意味着月供增加约280美元，年度还款成本增加超过3300美元。这对于已经面临高房价压力的首次购房者来说无疑是雪上加霜。

房地产经纪人协会数据显示，待售房屋签约量较上月下降15%，购房申请量更是暴跌25%。许多潜在买家选择推迟购房计划，等待市场环境改善。房屋建筑商股价普遍下跌10-15%，反映了市场对未来需求的悲观预期。

投资者面临多重挑战

房地产投资信托基金(REITs)遭受重创，住宅类REITs平均跌幅超过8%。高利率环境不仅增加了这些基金的融资成本，也降低了其相对于债券的吸引力。投资者需要重新评估房地产资产的配置权重。

对于房地产开发商而言，项目融资成本的上升可能导致部分开发计划延期或取消。这将进一步收紧未来的房屋供应，为长期房价提供支撑，但短期内需求疲软可能主导市场走势。

长期影响值得关注

历史数据显示，房贷利率每上升1个百分点，房屋销售量通常下降10-15%。如果当前利率水平持续，2024年房屋销售量可能降至2008年金融危机以来的最低水平。

然而，供应短缺仍是支撑房价的重要因素。美国房屋库存量仍处于历史低位，这意味着即使需求下降，房价大幅下跌的可能性相对较小。预计房价增速将从目前的6-8%放缓至2-3%。

对于持有房产的业主来说，再融资窗口基本关闭。那些在疫情期间锁定低利率的房主将更倾向于留在现有住房，进一步减少市场供应。

策略建议

购房者应考虑调整购房时机和预算。如果不是刚需，可考虑推迟6-12个月观察市场变化。对于必须购房的家庭，建议重点关注议价空间较大的房源。

投资者需要重新平衡投资组合。房地产相关资产的权重可能需要适当降低，同时关注那些现金流稳定、债务水平较低的优质REITs。

租赁市场可能因此受益。购房需求转向租赁需求将推高租金，为出租房产业主和租赁类REITs带来机会。

地缘政治风险的不确定性要求投资者保持更高的现金比例，以应对市场波动。同时密切关注油价变化和美联储政策信号，这些将是影响利率走势的关键因素。

Geopolitical Conflict Triggers Financial Market Chain Reaction

Escalating Iran-Israel tensions are triggering a ripple effect across the US real estate market. Investor concerns about Middle East geopolitical risks are driving safe-haven capital flows; however, contrary to traditional expectations, funds have not flowed heavily into US Treasury bonds. Instead, inflation expectations and rising oil prices have pushed up long-term interest rates.

30-year fixed mortgage rates have jumped from 6.8% last month to around 7.5%, marking the highest level since 2000. This sharp increase stems primarily from investor concerns about inflation resurging and expectations that the Federal Reserve may be forced to maintain higher interest rates for an extended period.

Homebuyers Under Pressure, Market Activity Plummets

Surging rates directly impact homebuyer affordability. For a $400,000 mortgage, a rate increase from 6.8% to 7.5% means monthly payments rise by approximately $280, with annual repayment costs increasing over $3,300. For first-time homebuyers already facing high home prices, this is salt in the wound.

Real Estate Board data shows pending home sales fell 15% month-over-month, while mortgage applications plunged 25%. Many potential buyers are postponing purchases, waiting for market conditions to improve. Homebuilder stocks have declined 10-15%, reflecting pessimistic expectations about future demand.

Investors Face Multiple Challenges

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) have been hit hard, with residential REITs experiencing average declines exceeding 8%. The high-rate environment not only increases financing costs for these funds but also reduces their attractiveness relative to bonds. Investors need to reassess their real estate asset allocation weights.

For real estate developers, rising project financing costs may lead to postponement or cancellation of some development plans. This will further tighten future housing supply, providing support for long-term home prices, but weak near-term demand may dominate market trends.

Long-Term Impacts Warrant Attention

Historical data shows that each 1 percentage point increase in mortgage rates typically reduces home sales by 10-15%. If current rate levels persist, 2024 home sales could fall to the lowest level since the 2008 financial crisis.

However, supply shortages remain a key factor supporting home prices. US housing inventory remains at historic lows, meaning even with declining demand, the probability of sharp price drops is relatively small. Home price appreciation is expected to slow from current 6-8% levels to 2-3%.

For homeowners, refinancing windows have essentially closed. Those who locked in low rates during the pandemic will be more inclined to stay in their current homes, further reducing market supply.

Strategic Recommendations

Homebuyers should consider adjusting timing and budgets. If not urgent, consider delaying 6-12 months to observe market changes. For families that must purchase, focus on properties with greater negotiating room.

Investors need to rebalance portfolios. Real estate asset weights may need appropriate reduction while focusing on high-quality REITs with stable cash flows and low debt levels.

The rental market may benefit. A shift from homebuying to rental demand will push up rents, creating opportunities for rental property owners and rental REITs.

Geopolitical uncertainty requires investors to maintain higher cash reserves to cope with market volatility. Meanwhile, closely monitor oil price changes and Federal Reserve policy signals, which will be key factors affecting interest rate trends.
