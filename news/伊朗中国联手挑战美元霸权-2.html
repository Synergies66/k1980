伊朗中国联手挑战美元霸权

Iran and China Join Forces to Challenge Dollar Hegemony

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月12日
伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，通过石油贸易去美元化等方式挑战美元国际储备货币地位。这一趋势可能重塑全球金融格局，影响美元汇率、大宗商品价格、投资组合配置，以及相关地区的就业和签证政策。投资者需关注货币多元化风险与机遇。
Iran and China are strengthening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region and challenging the US dollar's status as the international reserve currency through de-dollarization of oil trade. This trend may reshape the global financial landscape, affecting dollar exchange rates, commodity prices, portfolio allocation, and employment and visa policies in relevant regions. Investors need to monitor risks and opportunities in currency diversification.

地缘政治新格局正在形成

霍尔木兹海峡作为全球石油运输的咽喉要道，正成为挑战美元霸权的新战场。伊朗与中国在该地区的合作不仅限于能源贸易，更延伸至金融支付体系的重构。这标志着以美元为核心的国际货币体系面临前所未有的挑战。

中伊两国通过双边货币结算、建立替代性支付系统等方式，绕过以SWIFT为代表的西方金融基础设施。这种"去美元化"趋势正在全球范围内扩散，俄罗斯、印度等国也在积极参与类似安排。

对投资组合的直接冲击

对于投资者而言，这一变化带来多重影响。首先，美元的避险地位可能逐渐削弱，长期持有美元资产的风险增加。建议投资者考虑货币多元化配置，增加人民币、欧元等非美货币资产比重。

大宗商品市场将迎来重大变革。传统上以美元计价的石油、天然气等商品，可能出现更多以人民币或其他货币计价的交易。这为商品投资者提供了新的套利机会，但也增加了汇率风险。

能源板块投资价值重估不可避免。中国企业在伊朗能源领域的投资增加，相关上市公司股价可能受益。同时，传统石油美元体系受冲击，可能影响美国能源企业的国际竞争力。

就业市场的连锁反应

金融服务业首当其冲。国际贸易结算模式改变将减少对美元清算服务的需求，影响银行业相关岗位。但同时，人民币国际化进程加速将为中国金融机构创造更多海外业务机会。

跨境电商和国际贸易从业者需要适应多货币结算环境。掌握人民币、数字货币等新兴支付工具的专业人才将更受欢迎。企业财务部门也需要重新评估外汇风险管理策略。

能源行业就业格局面临重塑。中伊能源合作深化将为石油工程、海洋工程等领域专业人士提供新的海外工作机会，特别是具备中文或波斯语能力的技术人才。

签证政策调整预期

地缘政治重心转移可能影响各国签证政策。中国与伊朗商务往来增加，预计两国间商务签证申请将大幅增长。对于从事相关行业的专业人士，获得中伊两国工作签证的机会增加。

美国可能收紧对参与"去美元化"项目人员的签证审查。在伊朗或相关中国企业工作的经历可能成为美签申请的敏感因素。建议相关从业者提前做好职业规划和风险评估。

生活成本变化趋势

汇率波动将直接影响日常生活成本。美元相对疲软可能推高进口商品价格，特别是电子产品、汽车等美国品牌商品。但同时，国产替代品的竞争力增强，为消费者提供更多选择。

海外留学和旅游成本结构改变。赴美留学成本可能因美元地位变化而波动，而选择其他国家的性价比可能相对提升。投资移民者也需要重新评估各国货币的长期稳定性。

应对策略建议

个人投资者应建立多元化的货币资产配置，不要过度依赖单一货币。关注数字货币发展，特别是央行数字货币的国际化进程。

企业需要建立更灵活的外汇风险管理机制，考虑使用多种货币进行国际结算。积极了解人民币跨境支付系统等新兴金融基础设施。

职业发展方面，建议关注国际金融、跨境贸易、能源合作等领域的新机遇。学习相关语言技能和专业知识，为可能的职业转型做好准备。

A New Geopolitical Landscape is Forming

The Strait of Hormuz, as the jugular vein of global oil transportation, is becoming a new battleground for challenging dollar hegemony. Cooperation between Iran and China in this region extends beyond energy trade to the reconstruction of financial payment systems. This marks an unprecedented challenge to the international monetary system centered on the US dollar.

Through bilateral currency settlement and establishment of alternative payment systems, China and Iran are bypassing Western financial infrastructure represented by SWIFT. This "de-dollarization" trend is spreading globally, with countries like Russia and India actively participating in similar arrangements.

Direct Impact on Investment Portfolios

For investors, this change brings multiple effects. First, the safe-haven status of the US dollar may gradually weaken, increasing risks for long-term holders of dollar assets. It is recommended that investors consider diversified currency allocation and increase exposure to non-US currency assets such as the Chinese yuan and euro.

The commodities market will undergo major transformation. Commodities traditionally priced in dollars, such as oil and natural gas, may see more transactions priced in yuan or other currencies. This presents new arbitrage opportunities for commodity investors but also increases exchange rate risks.

Valuation reassessment of the energy sector is inevitable. Increased investment by Chinese enterprises in Iranian energy projects may benefit related listed companies. Meanwhile, the impact on the traditional petrodollar system may affect the international competitiveness of US energy companies.

Chain Reactions in the Job Market

The financial services industry will be hit first. Changes in international trade settlement patterns will reduce demand for dollar clearing services, affecting banking-related positions. Simultaneously, accelerated yuan internationalization will create more overseas business opportunities for Chinese financial institutions.

Cross-border e-commerce and international trade professionals need to adapt to multi-currency settlement environments. Professionals proficient in yuan, digital currencies, and other emerging payment tools will be increasingly sought after. Corporate finance departments also need to reassess foreign exchange risk management strategies.

The employment landscape in the energy industry faces restructuring. Deepened China-Iran energy cooperation will create new overseas job opportunities for professionals in petroleum engineering and marine engineering, particularly those with Chinese or Persian language skills.

Expected Adjustments in Visa Policies

Shifts in geopolitical focus may affect visa policies of various countries. Increased business exchanges between China and Iran are expected to significantly increase business visa applications between the two nations. Professionals in related industries will have increased opportunities to obtain work visas in both countries.

The United States may tighten visa scrutiny for those involved in "de-dollarization" projects. Work experience in Iran or with related Chinese enterprises may become a sensitive factor in US visa applications. It is recommended that relevant professionals conduct early career planning and risk assessment.

Trends in Cost of Living Changes

Exchange rate fluctuations will directly affect daily living costs. A relatively weaker dollar may push up prices of imported goods, especially US brand electronics and automobiles. Simultaneously, increased competitiveness of domestic alternatives provides consumers with more choices.

The cost structure for overseas education and tourism will change. The cost of studying in the US may fluctuate due to changes in the dollar's status, while the cost-effectiveness of choosing other countries may relatively improve. Those investing in immigration also need to reassess the long-term stability of various currencies.

Recommendations for Response Strategies

Individual investors should establish diversified multi-currency asset allocation and avoid over-reliance on a single currency. Pay attention to digital currency development, particularly the internationalization process of central bank digital currencies.

Enterprises need to establish more flexible foreign exchange risk management mechanisms and consider using multiple currencies for international settlements. Actively understand emerging financial infrastructure such as the yuan cross-border payment system.

Regarding career development, it is recommended to focus on new opportunities in international finance, cross-border trade, and energy cooperation. Learn relevant language skills and professional knowledge to prepare for potential career transitions.
