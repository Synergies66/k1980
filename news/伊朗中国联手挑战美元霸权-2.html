伊朗中国联手挑战美元霸权

Iran and China Join Forces to Challenge Dollar Hegemony

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月10日
伊朗和中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区展开去美元化合作，通过石油贸易本币结算、建立替代性金融体系等方式，直接冲击美元在国际贸易中的主导地位。这一举措可能引发全球金融格局重大变化，影响投资组合、汇率走势和国际贸易模式。
Iran and China are launching de-dollarization cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, challenging the dominance of the US dollar in international trade through methods such as settling oil trades in local currencies and establishing alternative financial systems. This initiative could trigger major changes in the global financial landscape, affecting investment portfolios, exchange rate trends, and international trade patterns.

去美元化浪潮席卷中东

在全球地缘政治紧张局势加剧的背景下，伊朗和中国正在霍尔木兹海峡这一全球石油运输要道展开深度合作，共同挑战美元在国际贸易中的霸主地位。这一战略合作不仅是对美国金融制裁的回应，更是对现有国际金融秩序的直接挑战。

石油贸易本币结算成关键突破口

中伊两国的合作核心集中在能源贸易领域。伊朗作为全球重要石油出口国，中国作为最大石油进口国，双方绕过美元进行石油交易具有天然优势。据业内消息，两国已开始使用人民币和伊朗里亚尔进行石油贸易结算，这直接减少了对美元的依赖。

这种做法的影响是深远的。传统上，国际石油贸易几乎完全依赖美元结算，这也是美元国际储备货币地位的重要支撑。中伊绕过美元交易的成功实践，可能激励更多国家采取类似策略。

替代性金融基础设施加速构建

为支撑去美元化交易，中伊两国正在构建独立于SWIFT系统的金融基础设施。中国的跨境银行间支付系统(CIPS)为人民币跨境结算提供了技术支撑，而伊朗也在积极对接这一系统。

此外，数字货币技术的应用也为两国提供了新的解决方案。央行数字货币(CBDC)的发展，使得跨境支付更加便捷，同时减少了对传统银行体系的依赖。

对全球投资者的直接影响

这一趋势对全球投资者产生多重影响。首先，美元汇率可能面临长期下行压力。随着更多国家减少美元使用，对美元的需求将逐渐下降，这将影响美元指数和相关投资产品的表现。

其次，大宗商品投资策略需要重新评估。传统的美元计价大宗商品价格体系可能发生变化，投资者需要关注以其他货币计价的商品期货市场发展。

对跨国企业运营的影响

跨国企业特别是能源、基建和贸易公司需要重新审视其风险管理策略。汇率对冲工具的选择将更加复杂，企业需要同时管理多种货币风险。

在中东地区有业务布局的企业，可能需要建立多元化的结算体系，既要维持与西方市场的美元结算传统，又要适应与中国、伊朗等国家的本币结算需求。

签证和移民政策连锁反应

金融合作的深化往往伴随着人员流动的增加。中伊两国可能在商务签证、技术人员交流等方面提供更多便利，为相关行业专业人士创造新的职业机会。

同时，随着两国经济联系加强，中文和波斯语人才需求可能增加，这为语言专业人士提供了新的职业方向。

未来趋势展望

专家分析认为，中伊去美元化合作只是全球金融格局变化的开始。更多受到美国制裁或希望降低美元依赖的国家，可能会加入这一行列。

然而，这一进程并非一帆风顺。美元霸权的形成历经数十年，其替代过程同样需要时间。投资者和企业需要在把握新机遇的同时，谨慎管理过渡期间的各种风险。

对于普通投资者而言，适度配置非美元资产，关注新兴市场货币和大宗商品投资机会，可能是应对这一长期趋势的明智选择。

De-dollarization Wave Sweeping the Middle East

Against the backdrop of escalating global geopolitical tensions, Iran and China are deepening cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical global oil transportation route, to jointly challenge the dominance of the US dollar in international trade. This strategic partnership is not only a response to American financial sanctions but also a direct challenge to the existing international financial order.

Local Currency Settlement in Oil Trade as Key Breakthrough

The core of Sino-Iranian cooperation focuses on the energy trade sector. As a major global oil exporter and China as the largest oil importer, both countries have natural advantages in bypassing the dollar for oil transactions. According to industry sources, the two countries have begun settling oil trades using the Chinese yuan and Iranian rial, directly reducing dependence on the US dollar.

The impact of this approach is far-reaching. Traditionally, international oil trade relies almost entirely on dollar settlements, which is an important pillar of the dollar's status as an international reserve currency. The successful practice of Sino-Iranian transactions bypassing the dollar may encourage more countries to adopt similar strategies.

Alternative Financial Infrastructure Accelerates Construction

To support de-dollarization transactions, China and Iran are building financial infrastructure independent of the SWIFT system. China's Cross-Border Interbank Payment System (CIPS) provides technical support for cross-border yuan settlements, while Iran is actively connecting to this system.

Additionally, the application of digital currency technology offers new solutions for both countries. The development of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) makes cross-border payments more convenient while reducing reliance on traditional banking systems.

Direct Impact on Global Investors

This trend has multiple impacts on global investors. First, the US dollar exchange rate may face long-term downward pressure. As more countries reduce dollar usage, demand for dollars will gradually decline, affecting the performance of the dollar index and related investment products.

Second, commodity investment strategies need reassessment. The traditional dollar-denominated commodity price system may change, and investors need to pay attention to the development of commodity futures markets priced in other currencies.

Impact on Multinational Enterprise Operations

Multinational enterprises, particularly those in energy, infrastructure, and trade sectors, need to reassess their risk management strategies. The selection of exchange rate hedging tools will become more complex, requiring enterprises to manage multiple currency risks simultaneously.

Enterprises with operations in the Middle East may need to establish diversified settlement systems, maintaining the traditional dollar settlements with Western markets while adapting to local currency settlement requirements with countries like China and Iran.

Chain Reactions in Visa and Immigration Policies

Deepening financial cooperation is often accompanied by increased human mobility. China and Iran may offer greater convenience in business visas, technical personnel exchanges, and other areas, creating new career opportunities for professionals in relevant industries.

Simultaneously, as economic ties between the two countries strengthen, demand for Chinese and Persian language talent may increase, providing new career directions for language professionals.

Future Outlook

Experts analyze that Sino-Iranian de-dollarization cooperation is just the beginning of changes in the global financial landscape. More countries facing US sanctions or seeking to reduce dollar dependence may join this trend.

However, this process will not be smooth sailing. Dollar hegemony has been established over decades, and its replacement will similarly require time. Investors and enterprises need to seize new opportunities while carefully managing various risks during the transition period.

For ordinary investors, moderately allocating non-dollar assets and paying attention to emerging market currencies and commodity investment opportunities may be a prudent strategy to cope with this long-term trend.
