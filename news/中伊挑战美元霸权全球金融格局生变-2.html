中伊挑战美元霸权，全球金融格局生变

China and Iran Challenge US Dollar Hegemony, Global Financial Landscape Shifts

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月14日
伊朗与中国在霍尔木兹海峡地区加强合作，推动去美元化进程。这一举措可能重塑国际贸易结算体系，影响全球投资配置、汇率走势和地缘政治格局。对投资者而言，需关注新兴货币机遇与美元资产风险；对企业来说，多元化结算方式成为必然趋势。
Iran and China strengthen cooperation in the Strait of Hormuz region, advancing de-dollarization efforts. This initiative may reshape the international trade settlement system, affecting global investment allocation, exchange rate trends, and geopolitical landscape. Investors need to monitor emerging currency opportunities and risks to dollar assets; for enterprises, diversifying settlement methods becomes an inevitable trend.

霍尔木兹海峡：新金融战场的崛起

霍尔木兹海峡作为全球石油运输的咽喉要道，如今正成为挑战美元霸权的新战场。伊朗与中国在这一战略要地的金融合作，标志着去美元化进程进入新阶段。这不仅是两国间的双边合作，更是对现有国际金融秩序的直接挑战。

对个人投资的直接冲击

对普通投资者而言，这一变化带来多重影响。首先，美元的避险地位可能受到冲击，长期持有美元资产需要重新评估风险。其次，人民币、黄金等替代性资产的配置价值上升，建议适度增加此类资产比重。

石油价格波动性可能加剧，能源类投资需要更加谨慎。同时，受益于去美元化的区域性银行、支付系统公司值得关注，这些企业可能迎来新的发展机遇。

企业运营策略调整

对于从事国际贸易的企业，结算货币多元化已成必然趋势。依赖中东能源进口的公司需要考虑建立多货币结算体系，降低汇率风险。跨国公司应当评估其在不同货币区域的资产配置，避免过度集中于美元体系。

中小企业可以考虑通过数字货币或区域性支付系统参与国际贸易，这些新兴渠道可能提供更低的交易成本和更高的效率。

签证与移民政策变化

地缘政治格局的变化可能影响各国移民政策。美国可能收紧对某些地区的签证政策，而中国、伊朗等国可能对特定技能人才更加开放。金融科技、能源技术等领域的专业人士可能面临更多机遇。

考虑移民的人士需要关注目标国家的货币政策走向，选择经济韧性较强、不过度依赖美元体系的国家可能更具前瞻性。

生活成本与消费影响

去美元化进程可能推高通胀水平，特别是在能源和大宗商品领域。消费者需要为可能的价格上涨做好准备，适当增加必需品储备。同时，汇率波动可能影响进口商品价格，海外购物、留学等成本需要重新计算。

长期趋势与应对策略

这一变化反映了多极化世界的发展趋势。投资者应当适应更加复杂的国际环境，建立多元化的资产配置策略。企业需要增强风险管理能力，建立更加灵活的运营模式。

个人层面，提升对国际金融知识的了解，关注新兴支付技术和投资工具，将有助于在变化中把握机遇。同时，保持资产配置的国际化和多元化，是应对不确定性的最佳策略。

结语

中伊在霍尔木兹海峡的合作只是开始，去美元化将是一个长期过程。这既带来挑战，也孕育机遇。关键是要保持敏锐的洞察力，及时调整个人和企业策略，在变革中寻找新的增长点。

Strait of Hormuz: Rise of a New Financial Battlefield

As the lifeline of global oil transportation, the Strait of Hormuz is now becoming a new battleground for challenging US dollar hegemony. Financial cooperation between Iran and China in this strategic location marks a new phase in the de-dollarization process. This is not merely bilateral cooperation between the two nations, but a direct challenge to the existing international financial order.

Direct Impact on Personal Investment

For ordinary investors, this change brings multiple impacts. First, the safe-haven status of the US dollar may be compromised, requiring reassessment of risks associated with long-term holdings of dollar assets. Second, the allocation value of alternative assets such as the Chinese yuan and gold is rising, suggesting a moderate increase in the proportion of such assets.

Oil price volatility may intensify, requiring more cautious approaches to energy investments. Meanwhile, regional banks and payment system companies benefiting from de-dollarization deserve attention, as these enterprises may encounter new development opportunities.

Enterprise Operational Strategy Adjustments

For enterprises engaged in international trade, currency diversification in settlements has become an inevitable trend. Companies relying on energy imports from the Middle East need to consider establishing multi-currency settlement systems to reduce exchange rate risks. Multinational corporations should evaluate their asset allocation across different currency zones to avoid over-concentration in the dollar system.

Small and medium-sized enterprises may consider participating in international trade through digital currencies or regional payment systems, which could offer lower transaction costs and higher efficiency.

Changes in Visa and Immigration Policies

Shifts in geopolitical landscape may affect immigration policies of various countries. The United States may tighten visa policies toward certain regions, while countries like China and Iran may become more welcoming to professionals with specific skill sets. Professionals in fintech and energy technology sectors may face more opportunities.

Those considering immigration should pay attention to the monetary policy trajectory of target countries, with preference for nations demonstrating strong economic resilience and not overly dependent on the dollar system.

Impact on Living Costs and Consumption

The de-dollarization process may push up inflation levels, particularly in energy and commodities sectors. Consumers should prepare for potential price increases and appropriately stockpile necessities. Meanwhile, exchange rate fluctuations may affect import commodity prices, requiring recalculation of costs for overseas shopping, overseas education, and similar expenses.

Long-term Trends and Response Strategies

This change reflects the development trend of a multipolar world. Investors should adapt to a more complex international environment and establish diversified asset allocation strategies. Enterprises need to strengthen risk management capabilities and establish more flexible operational models.

At the personal level, enhancing understanding of international finance, paying attention to emerging payment technologies and investment tools will help seize opportunities in this changing landscape. Simultaneously, maintaining internationalized and diversified asset allocation is the best strategy for dealing with uncertainty.

Conclusion

Cooperation between China and Iran in the Strait of Hormuz is merely the beginning; de-dollarization will be a long-term process. This brings both challenges and opportunities. The key is to maintain keen insight, timely adjust personal and enterprise strategies, and seek new growth points amid this transformation.
