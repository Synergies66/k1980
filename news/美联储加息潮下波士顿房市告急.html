美联储加息潮下波士顿房市告急

Boston Housing Market in Crisis as Fed Raises Rates

K1980 深度 · 2026年04月13日
随着抵押贷款利率飙升至7%以上，大波士顿地区房地产市场陷入深度调整，房屋销量骤降30%，价格开始松动。这一趋势预计将持续至明年春季，对购房者、投资者和租房市场产生深远影响。高科技从业者和留学生群体面临置业时机选择难题。
As mortgage interest rates soar above 7%, the Greater Boston real estate market has entered a deep adjustment period with home sales plummeting 30% and prices beginning to soften. This trend is expected to persist through spring next year, with far-reaching impacts on homebuyers, investors, and the rental market. Tech workers and international students face difficult timing decisions for homeownership.

利率飙升重创波士顿房市

大波士顿地区房地产市场正经历2008年金融危机以来最严重的调整。30年期固定抵押贷款利率从去年的3%左右飙升至目前的7.2%，这意味着同样一套50万美元的房屋，月供增加了近1000美元。对于年收入10-15万美元的中产家庭而言，购房门槛大幅提高。

房屋销量数据显示，10月份大波士顿地区成交量同比下降32%，库存开始积累。剑桥、牛顿等传统热门区域的房屋平均上市时间延长至45天，较去年同期翻倍。

科技从业者购房策略生变

Google、微软、亚马逊等科技巨头在波士顿的大量员工原本是房市主力军。然而面对高利率环境，即便是年薪20万美元以上的软件工程师也开始重新考虑购房时机。许多人选择继续租房观望，或将目光转向康涅狄格州等周边地区。

对于持H1B签证的华人科技从业者，购房决策更加复杂。一方面担心错过市场底部，另一方面又面临身份不确定性。建议这部分群体重点关注利率走势，若美联储明年开始降息，可考虑锁定较低利率再入市。

投资者机会与风险并存

房地产投资信托基金(REITs)和个人投资者面临分化行情。商业地产REITs受远程办公冲击持续承压，而专注出租公寓的REITs可能受益于购房需求向租房的转移。

现金充裕的投资者或将迎来机会窗口。随着开发商资金压力增大，部分新建项目可能以折扣价出售。但需警惕波士顿人口流出风险——高生活成本正迫使部分居民迁往南部和中西部城市。

留学生租房市场升温

哈佛、MIT等名校周边租房需求激增。原本计划毕业后购房的中国留学生大多推迟置业计划，继续租房或与室友合租。预计2024年春季学期租金将上涨8-12%。

建议在读研究生提前6个月开始看房，并考虑签订较长租期以锁定租金。对于计划留美工作的博士生和访问学者，当前可关注法拍房和银行拥有房产(REO)机会。

政策影响与未来展望

马萨诸塞州政府正考虑放宽分区限制，允许更多多户型住宅建设。联邦层面，拜登政府的首次购房者税收抵免政策可能在2024年重新推出。

美联储政策转向是关键变量。若通胀持续回落，预计2024年下半年利率开始下降，届时被压抑的购房需求将集中释放。但考虑到波士顿供给严重不足的结构性问题，房价大幅下跌概率较低。

实用建议

购房者应做好长期规划，避免被短期市场波动影响决策。有稳定收入和首付能力的刚需买家，可考虑选择可调整利率抵押贷款(ARM)，以降低初期还款压力。投资者则应重点关注现金流为正的出租物业，避免过度杠杆。

Rising Rates Devastate Boston Housing Market

The Greater Boston real estate market is experiencing its most severe adjustment since the 2008 financial crisis. Thirty-year fixed mortgage rates have surged from around 3% last year to 7.2% currently, meaning the monthly payment for the same $500,000 house has increased by nearly $1,000. For middle-class families earning $100,000-$150,000 annually, the homebuying threshold has risen sharply.

Home sales data shows that October transactions in Greater Boston declined 32% year-over-year, with inventory beginning to accumulate. In traditional hot markets like Cambridge and Newton, average listing times have extended to 45 days, doubling compared to the same period last year.

Tech Workers Reassess Homebuying Strategies

Employees of tech giants like Google, Microsoft, and Amazon were previously the backbone of Boston's housing market. However, facing high interest rates, even software engineers earning over $200,000 annually are reconsidering their homebuying timelines. Many are choosing to continue renting and wait, or looking toward neighboring areas like Connecticut.

For Chinese tech workers holding H1B visas, homebuying decisions are even more complex. They worry about missing the market bottom while facing immigration status uncertainties. This group should focus on interest rate trends; if the Federal Reserve begins cutting rates next year, they should consider locking in lower rates before entering the market.

Mixed Opportunities and Risks for Investors

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) and individual investors face divergent conditions. Commercial real estate REITs continue to suffer from remote work impacts, while REITs focused on rental apartments may benefit from the shift of demand from buying to renting.

Cash-rich investors may face a window of opportunity. As developers face mounting financial pressure, some new projects may be sold at discounts. However, caution is warranted regarding Boston's population outflow risk—high living costs are driving some residents to migrate to southern and midwest cities.

International Student Rental Market Heats Up

Rental demand is surging around top schools like Harvard and MIT. Chinese graduate students who originally planned to purchase homes after graduation are mostly postponing homeownership plans and continuing to rent or share accommodations. Rents are expected to rise 8-12% in spring 2024 semester.

Current graduate students should start looking for housing six months in advance and consider signing longer leases to lock in rental rates. For doctoral students and visiting scholars planning to work in the US, current opportunities exist in foreclosed properties and bank-owned real estate (REO).

Policy Impact and Future Outlook

The Massachusetts government is considering relaxing zoning restrictions to allow more multi-family housing construction. At the federal level, the Biden administration's first-time homebuyer tax credit may be reintroduced in 2024.

The Federal Reserve's policy shift is the key variable. If inflation continues to decline, interest rate reductions are expected to begin in the second half of 2024, when suppressed homebuying demand will be released concentrated. However, given Boston's structural housing shortage, the probability of significant price declines is relatively low.

Practical Recommendations

Homebuyers should make long-term plans and avoid being swayed by short-term market fluctuations. First-time buyers with stable income and down payment capacity may consider adjustable-rate mortgages (ARMs) to reduce initial repayment pressure. Investors should focus on rental properties with positive cash flow and avoid excessive leverage.
